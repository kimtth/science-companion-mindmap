# General Biology - Comprehensive Overview

## üéØ Purpose
This document provides a comprehensive overview of college-level biology, covering molecular mechanisms to ecosystem dynamics, including key concepts, formulas, and applications. It serves as both a conceptual guide and technical reference for understanding the fundamental principles governing living systems.

## üìö Main Topics

### 1. Cell Biology
The study of cells, the basic unit of life:
- **Cell Structure**: Organization of prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells
- **Membrane Structure & Transport**: Cell boundaries and material exchange
- **Cell Metabolism**: Energy production through respiration and photosynthesis
- **Cell Division**: Mitosis and meiosis for growth and reproduction
- **Cell Communication**: Signal transduction pathways

**Key Applications**: Medicine, biotechnology, cancer research, drug development

### 2. Genetics
The science of heredity and genetic variation:
- **DNA Structure**: Double helix and genetic information storage
- **Gene Expression**: From DNA to RNA to proteins (Central Dogma)
- **Mendelian Inheritance**: Patterns of trait transmission
- **Molecular Genetics**: Gene regulation, mutations, and biotechnology

**Key Applications**: Genetic counseling, gene therapy, GMO development, forensics, personalized medicine

### 3. Evolution
The unifying principle of biology:
- **Natural Selection**: Mechanism of adaptive evolution
- **Evidence for Evolution**: Fossils, comparative anatomy, molecular data
- **Speciation**: Formation of new species
- **Population Genetics**: Allele frequencies and Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium

**Key Applications**: Medicine (antibiotic resistance), agriculture, conservation, understanding biodiversity

### 4. Ecology
Interactions between organisms and their environment:
- **Population Ecology**: Population dynamics and growth
- **Community Ecology**: Species interactions (competition, predation, symbiosis)
- **Ecosystem Ecology**: Energy flow and nutrient cycling
- **Conservation Biology**: Protecting biodiversity

**Key Applications**: Conservation, wildlife management, agriculture, climate change mitigation, sustainability

### 5. Animal & Plant Biology
Structure and function of organisms:
- **Plant Structure**: Roots, stems, leaves adapted for photosynthesis
- **Plant Reproduction**: Flowers, pollination, seeds
- **Animal Organ Systems**: Circulatory, nervous, digestive, immune systems
- **Homeostasis**: Maintaining internal stability

**Key Applications**: Medicine, agriculture, veterinary science, pharmacology

## üîó Interconnections
- Cell biology provides molecular foundation for all other areas
- Genetics explains inheritance patterns and evolution mechanisms
- Evolution connects all living organisms through common ancestry
- Ecology studies interactions at population and ecosystem levels
- Animal/plant biology applies cellular and genetic principles to whole organisms

## üî¨ Molecular Biology Concepts

### DNA Structure & Replication
- **Double helix**: Two antiparallel strands with A-T (2 H-bonds) and G-C (3 H-bonds) base pairing
- **Chargaff's rules**: %A = %T and %G = %C
- **Semiconservative replication**: Each strand serves as template
- **Key enzymes**: Helicase (unwinds), DNA polymerase (synthesizes 5'‚Üí3'), ligase (joins fragments)
- **Leading vs lagging strands**: Continuous vs discontinuous (Okazaki fragments)

### Gene Expression
- **Transcription**: DNA ‚Üí mRNA by RNA polymerase (5'‚Üí3' synthesis)
  - Promoter: RNA polymerase binding site (TATA box)
  - Template strand read 3'‚Üí5'
- **RNA processing (eukaryotes)**: 5' cap, 3' poly-A tail, splicing (introns removed)
- **Translation**: mRNA ‚Üí protein at ribosomes
  - Genetic code: 64 codons (61 amino acids + 3 stop: UAA, UAG, UGA)
  - Start codon: AUG (methionine)
  - tRNA anticodons pair with mRNA codons

### Gene Regulation
- **Prokaryotes**: Operons (lac and trp)
  - Negative control: Repressor blocks transcription
  - Positive control: Activator promotes transcription
- **Eukaryotes**: 
  - Chromatin remodeling (histone acetylation)
  - Transcription factors
  - Enhancers and silencers
  - Epigenetics: DNA methylation, histone modification

### Mutations
- **Point mutations**: Silent, missense, nonsense
- **Frameshift**: Insertion/deletion (not multiple of 3)
- **Chromosomal**: Deletion, duplication, inversion, translocation

## üß™ Cell Biology

### Cell Membrane & Transport
- **Fluid mosaic model**: Phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins
- **Passive transport**: No ATP required
  - Simple diffusion, facilitated diffusion, osmosis
- **Active transport**: Requires ATP (Na‚Å∫/K‚Å∫ pump)
- **Bulk transport**: Endocytosis, exocytosis
- **Tonicity**: Isotonic, hypertonic (crenation), hypotonic (lysis/turgor)

### Cellular Respiration
**Overall equation**: $C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2 \rightarrow 6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{ATP}$

- **Glycolysis** (cytoplasm): Glucose ‚Üí 2 pyruvate; Net: 2 ATP, 2 NADH
- **Pyruvate oxidation**: 2 pyruvate ‚Üí 2 acetyl-CoA + 2 CO‚ÇÇ; 2 NADH
- **Krebs cycle** (mitochondrial matrix): Per glucose: 2 ATP, 6 NADH, 2 FADH‚ÇÇ, 4 CO‚ÇÇ
- **Electron transport chain**: ~32-34 ATP via oxidative phosphorylation
- **Total**: ~36-38 ATP per glucose

### Photosynthesis
**Overall equation**: $6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{light} \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6O_2$

- **Light reactions** (thylakoid): Light ‚Üí ATP + NADPH
  - Water splitting: $2H_2O \rightarrow 4H^+ + O_2 + 4e^-$
  - Non-cyclic: ATP + NADPH; Cyclic: ATP only
- **Calvin cycle** (stroma): CO‚ÇÇ fixation
  - Rubisco fixes CO‚ÇÇ to RuBP ‚Üí 3-PGA
  - 3 CO‚ÇÇ ‚Üí 1 G3P (3-carbon sugar)
  - 6 turns to make 1 glucose

### Fermentation
- **Alcohol**: Pyruvate ‚Üí ethanol + CO‚ÇÇ
- **Lactic acid**: Pyruvate ‚Üí lactate
- **Net**: 2 ATP per glucose (glycolysis only, regenerates NAD‚Å∫)

### Cell Division
- **Mitosis**: 2 identical diploid cells (Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase, Cytokinesis)
- **Meiosis**: 4 non-identical haploid gametes
  - Meiosis I: Homologous chromosomes separate (crossing over)
  - Meiosis II: Sister chromatids separate
- **Variation sources**: Crossing over, independent assortment, random fertilization

## üß† Genetics

### Mendelian Genetics
- **Law of Segregation**: Alleles separate during gamete formation
- **Law of Independent Assortment**: Different genes assort independently
- **Dominant vs recessive**: Expressed in heterozygotes vs homozygotes only
- **Punnett squares**: Predict offspring genotypes and phenotypes
  - Monohybrid: 3:1 ratio; Dihybrid: 9:3:3:1 ratio

### Beyond Mendel
- **Incomplete dominance**: Heterozygote intermediate phenotype
- **Codominance**: Both alleles expressed (ABO blood type)
- **Multiple alleles**: >2 alleles in population
- **Pleiotropy**: One gene affects multiple traits
- **Epistasis**: One gene masks another
- **Polygenic inheritance**: Multiple genes affect one trait (height, skin color)

### Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium
**Allele frequencies**: $p + q = 1$
**Genotype frequencies**: $p^2 + 2pq + q^2 = 1$
- $p^2$ = frequency of AA
- $2pq$ = frequency of Aa
- $q^2$ = frequency of aa
- **Assumptions**: No mutation, random mating, no selection, no gene flow, large population
- **Use**: Detect if evolution is occurring

## üå± Evolution

### Natural Selection
- **Variation**: Individuals differ in traits
- **Heritability**: Traits passed to offspring
- **Differential reproduction**: Some survive/reproduce better
- **Result**: Allele frequencies change over time

### Types of Selection
- **Directional**: Favors one extreme
- **Stabilizing**: Favors intermediate
- **Disruptive**: Favors both extremes

### Evidence for Evolution
1. Fossil record (transitional forms)
2. Comparative anatomy (homologous/analogous structures)
3. Comparative embryology
4. Molecular biology (DNA/protein similarities)
5. Biogeography (species distribution)

### Speciation
- **Allopatric**: Geographic isolation
- **Sympatric**: No geographic barrier (polyploidy)
- **Reproductive isolation**: Prezygotic and postzygotic barriers

### Population Genetics
- **Genetic drift**: Random changes (bottleneck, founder effects)
- **Gene flow**: Allele movement between populations
- **Mutation**: Ultimate source of variation

## üå≥ Diversity of Life

### Three Domains
1. **Bacteria**: Prokaryotes, peptidoglycan cell walls
2. **Archaea**: Prokaryotes, extremophiles
3. **Eukarya**: Protists, fungi, plants, animals

### Viruses
- Non-cellular infectious agents
- Nucleic acid (DNA/RNA) in protein coat
- Lytic cycle (immediate reproduction) vs lysogenic cycle (dormant)
- Require host cells

### Plant Diversity
- **Bryophytes**: Mosses, liverworts (no vascular tissue)
- **Seedless vascular**: Ferns
- **Gymnosperms**: Conifers (naked seeds in cones)
- **Angiosperms**: Flowering plants (seeds in fruits)

### Animal Diversity
- **Invertebrates** (95% of species): Sponges, cnidarians, mollusks, annelids, arthropods, echinoderms
- **Vertebrates**: Fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals

### Water Transport

- **Auxins**: Cell elongation, apical dominance
- **Cytokinins**: Cell division
- **Gibberellins**: Stem elongation, germination
- **Abscisic acid**: Stomatal closure, dormancy
- **Ethylene**: Fruit ripening, senescence

## ü¶Ü Animal Biology

### Major Organ Systems

**Circulatory System**
- Open vs closed circulation
- Single (fish) vs double (mammals) circulation
- Heart chambers: 2 (fish), 3 (amphibians), 4 (mammals/birds)

**Respiratory System**
- Gas exchange: Fick's law
- Gills (countercurrent exchange) vs lungs (alveoli)
- Hemoglobin: Cooperative O‚ÇÇ binding, Bohr shift

**Digestive System**
- Mouth ‚Üí esophagus ‚Üí stomach (pH 2, pepsin) ‚Üí small intestine (main absorption) ‚Üí large intestine (water absorption)

**Nervous System**
- **Neurons**: Dendrites ‚Üí cell body ‚Üí axon ‚Üí terminals
- **Resting potential**: ~-70 mV (Na‚Å∫/K‚Å∫ pump)
- **Action potential**: Threshold (-55 mV) ‚Üí depolarization (+40 mV) ‚Üí repolarization
- **Synapse**: Neurotransmitter communication
- **Brain regions**: Cerebrum, cerebellum, brainstem

**Endocrine System**
- Hormones: Chemical messengers
- Steroid (lipid-soluble) vs peptide (water-soluble)
- Negative feedback (homeostasis) vs positive feedback (rare)

**Immune System**
- **Innate**: Non-specific (barriers, phagocytes, inflammation)
- **Adaptive**: Specific (B cells/antibodies, T cells)
- **Clonal selection**: Antigen-specific proliferation
- **Memory cells**: Rapid secondary response

## üåç Ecology

### Energy Flow
- **Trophic levels**: Producers ‚Üí primary consumers ‚Üí secondary consumers ‚Üí decomposers
- **10% rule**: ~10% energy transferred between levels
- **Biomass pyramid**: Decreases at higher levels

### Nutrient Cycling
- **Carbon cycle**: Photosynthesis ‚Üî respiration; fossil fuels
- **Nitrogen cycle**: N‚ÇÇ fixation ‚Üí NH‚ÇÉ/NH‚ÇÑ‚Å∫ ‚Üí NO‚ÇÇ‚Åª ‚Üí NO‚ÇÉ‚Åª ‚Üí denitrification
- **Phosphorus cycle**: Weathering, no atmospheric component
- **Water cycle**: Evaporation ‚Üí condensation ‚Üí precipitation

### Population Ecology
**Exponential growth**: $\frac{dN}{dt} = r_{max}N$ (J-shaped)
**Logistic growth**: $\frac{dN}{dt} = r_{max}N\left(\frac{K-N}{K}\right)$ (S-shaped)
- K = carrying capacity
- **Density-dependent**: Competition, predation, disease
- **Density-independent**: Weather, disasters
- **Life history**: r-selected (many offspring) vs K-selected (few offspring, parental care)

### Community Ecology
- **Competition**: Both species harmed; competitive exclusion
- **Predation**: Consumer-resource; coevolution
- **Symbiosis**: Mutualism (+/+), commensalism (+/0), parasitism (+/-)
- **Keystone species**: Disproportionate impact
- **Succession**: Primary (bare rock) vs secondary (disturbance)

### Conservation
- **Threats**: Habitat loss, invasive species, pollution, climate change, overexploitation
- **Strategies**: Protected areas, restoration, captive breeding

## üßÆ Key Formulas & Calculations

### Hardy-Weinberg
$p^2 + 2pq + q^2 = 1$ and $p + q = 1$

### Population Growth
- **Exponential**: $N_t = N_0 e^{rt}$
- **Doubling time**: $t_d = \frac{0.693}{r}$

### Water Potential
$\Psi = \Psi_p + \Psi_s$ where $\Psi_s = -iCRT$

### Chi-Square Test
$\chi^2 = \sum \frac{(O - E)^2}{E}$

### Primary Productivity
- **NPP** = GPP - respiration
- **Energy to consumers**: ~10% of NPP
