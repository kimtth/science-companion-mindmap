# Biology Terminology

## Core Concepts from Biology Mindmaps

### Cell Biology
- **Cell**: Basic structural and functional unit of all living organisms
- **Organelle**: Specialized membrane-bound structure within a cell performing specific functions
- **Cell Structure**: Organization and arrangement of cellular components
- **Membrane**: Phospholipid bilayer that encloses cells and organelles
- **Transport**: Movement of substances across cell membranes (active or passive)
- **Metabolism**: Sum of all chemical reactions occurring within a living organism
- **Cell Division**: Process by which a cell divides into two daughter cells (mitosis or meiosis)
- **Cell Signaling**: Communication between cells through chemical signals
- **Prokaryote**: Single-celled organism without a membrane-bound nucleus (bacteria, archaea)
- **Eukaryote**: Organism whose cells contain a membrane-bound nucleus and organelles

#### Prokaryotic Cell Structures
- **Nucleoid**: DNA region without membrane in prokaryotes; contains circular chromosome
- **Plasmid**: Small circular DNA molecule in bacteria; carries additional genes
- **Peptidoglycan**: Sugar-amino acid polymer in bacterial cell walls; target of antibiotics
- **Gram-Positive**: Bacteria with thick peptidoglycan layer; stains purple
- **Gram-Negative**: Bacteria with thin peptidoglycan and outer membrane; stains pink
- **Capsule**: Polysaccharide layer outside cell wall; provides protection and adhesion
- **Pili**: Hair-like appendages on bacteria; used for attachment and conjugation
- **Flagellar Motor**: Proton-driven rotary motor in bacterial flagella
- **Flagellin**: Structural protein forming bacterial flagellar filament
- **70S Ribosome**: Smaller prokaryotic ribosome (50S + 30S subunits)

#### Eukaryotic Organelles
- **Nuclear Envelope**: Double membrane surrounding nucleus; regulates nuclear-cytoplasmic exchange
- **Nuclear Pores**: Selective gateways in nuclear envelope; transport proteins and RNA
- **Chromatin**: DNA-protein complex; condenses into chromosomes during division
- **Nucleolus**: Region within nucleus; site of ribosome assembly and rRNA synthesis
- **Mitochondrial Matrix**: Inner compartment of mitochondria; location of Krebs cycle
- **Cristae**: Inner membrane folds in mitochondria; increases surface area for electron transport
- **Mitochondrial DNA**: Circular chromosome in mitochondria; maternally inherited
- **ATP Synthase**: Molecular motor in mitochondria; produces ATP from proton gradient
- **Thylakoid**: Membrane sacs in chloroplasts; site of light reactions
- **Stroma**: Fluid-filled space in chloroplasts; location of Calvin cycle
- **Chloroplast DNA**: Circular chromosome in chloroplasts; encodes some proteins
- **Grana**: Stacked thylakoids in chloroplasts; maximize light absorption
- **Rough ER**: Endoplasmic reticulum with ribosomes; protein synthesis and processing
- **Smooth ER**: ER without ribosomes; lipid synthesis and detoxification
- **Protein Folding**: Process of achieving proper 3D structure; assisted by chaperones
- **Glycosylation**: Addition of sugar chains to proteins; post-translational modification
- **Cis Face**: Receiving side of Golgi apparatus; accepts vesicles from ER
- **Trans Face**: Shipping side of Golgi apparatus; sends vesicles to destinations
- **Hydrolytic Enzymes**: Digestive enzymes in lysosomes; function at acidic pH
- **Autophagy**: Self-digestion process; recycles damaged organelles
- **Peroxisome**: Organelle performing oxidative reactions; breaks down fatty acids and H₂O₂
- **Vacuole**: Storage organelle; large in plant cells, maintains turgor pressure

#### Cytoskeleton Components
- **Microfilaments**: Actin filaments (7nm); involved in cell movement and shape
- **Actin-Myosin**: Contractile system; powers muscle contraction and cytokinesis
- **Intermediate Filaments**: 8-10nm diameter; provide mechanical strength
- **Microtubules**: Tubulin polymers (25nm); tracks for motor proteins
- **Centrioles**: Cylindrical structures; organize microtubules and spindle apparatus
- **Cilia**: Short hair-like projections; move fluid over cell surface
- **Flagella (Eukaryotic)**: Long whip-like structures; 9+2 microtubule arrangement
- **Kinesin**: Motor protein; moves cargo toward plus end of microtubules
- **Dynein**: Motor protein; moves cargo toward minus end of microtubules

### Genetics
- **DNA**: Deoxyribonucleic acid; genetic material encoding hereditary information
- **Gene**: Segment of DNA that codes for a specific protein or functional RNA
- **Gene Expression**: Process by which genetic information is used to synthesize proteins
- **Transcription**: Synthesis of RNA from a DNA template
- **Translation**: Synthesis of proteins from mRNA template at ribosomes
- **Replication**: Process of copying DNA to produce identical DNA molecules
- **Mendelian Inheritance**: Patterns of heredity following Mendel's laws
- **Allele**: Alternative form of a gene at a specific locus
- **Genotype**: Genetic makeup of an organism
- **Phenotype**: Observable characteristics of an organism
- **Mutation**: Change in DNA sequence
- **Gene Regulation**: Control of gene expression levels

### Evolution
- **Natural Selection**: Process where organisms better adapted to their environment survive and reproduce
- **Adaptation**: Trait that increases an organism's fitness in its environment
- **Speciation**: Formation of new and distinct species through evolution
- **Fossil Record**: Collection of fossils documenting the history of life on Earth
- **Molecular Evidence**: DNA/protein sequence similarities supporting common ancestry
- **Common Ancestry**: Evolutionary principle that all life shares a common origin
- **Fitness**: Reproductive success of an organism in its environment
- **Population Genetics**: Study of genetic variation and change within populations
- **Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium**: Baseline model for allele frequencies in non-evolving populations
- **Genetic Drift**: Random changes in allele frequencies in small populations
- **Gene Flow**: Movement of genes between populations through migration

### Ecology
- **Ecosystem**: Community of living organisms interacting with their physical environment
- **Population**: Group of individuals of the same species living in the same area
- **Community**: All populations of different species living together in an area
- **Population Dynamics**: Changes in population size and composition over time
- **Population Growth**: Increase in population size over time (exponential or logistic)
- **Energy Flow**: Transfer of energy through trophic levels in an ecosystem
- **Nutrient Cycling**: Movement and recycling of nutrients through ecosystems
- **Food Web**: Complex network of feeding relationships in an ecosystem
- **Trophic Level**: Position in a food chain (producer, primary consumer, etc.)
- **Carrying Capacity**: Maximum population size an environment can sustain
- **Succession**: Predictable changes in community composition over time
- **Biome**: Large-scale ecosystem characterized by distinct climate and vegetation

### Animal & Plant Biology
- **Plant Structure**: Organization of plant tissues and organs (roots, stems, leaves)
- **Plant Reproduction**: Sexual and asexual reproduction mechanisms in plants
- **Animal Organ Systems**: Integrated systems (circulatory, respiratory, digestive, etc.)
- **Homeostasis**: Maintenance of stable internal conditions despite external changes
- **Photosynthesis**: Process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy
- **Transpiration**: Water movement through plants and evaporation from leaves
- **Pollination**: Transfer of pollen from male to female plant structures
- **Vascular Tissue**: Transport tissues in plants (xylem and phloem)

### Diversity of Life
- **Virus**: Non-cellular infectious agent requiring a host cell for replication
- **Prokaryote**: Organism lacking a membrane-bound nucleus (bacteria, archaea)
- **Protist**: Diverse group of eukaryotic organisms (mostly unicellular)
- **Fungi**: Eukaryotic organisms including yeasts, molds, and mushrooms
- **Phylogeny**: Evolutionary history and relationships among organisms
- **Taxonomy**: Science of classifying and naming organisms
- **Domain**: Highest taxonomic rank (Bacteria, Archaea, Eukarya)
- **Kingdom**: Major taxonomic category below domain
- **Species**: Group of organisms capable of interbreeding and producing fertile offspring
- **Biodiversity**: Variety of life forms in an ecosystem or on Earth
- **Adaptation**: Evolutionary process resulting in traits suited to environment

## Key Processes

#### Membrane Transport
- **Fluid Mosaic Model**: Dynamic membrane structure with proteins floating in lipid bilayer
- **Phospholipid**: Amphipathic molecule with hydrophilic head and hydrophobic tails
- **Integral Protein**: Protein embedded in membrane; has transmembrane domains
- **Peripheral Protein**: Protein attached to membrane surface; easily removed
- **Channel Protein**: Forms aqueous pore for selective ion passage
- **Carrier Protein**: Undergoes conformational change to transport specific molecules
- **Cholesterol**: Membrane fluidity regulator; prevents crystallization
- **Glycocalyx**: Carbohydrate coating on cell surface; cell recognition and protection
- **Simple Diffusion**: Movement down concentration gradient; no energy required
- **Facilitated Diffusion**: Protein-mediated passive transport; specific for molecules
- **Osmosis**: Water diffusion across membrane; critical for volume regulation
- **Isotonic**: Solution with equal solute concentration; no net water movement
- **Hypotonic**: Solution with lower solute outside; water enters cell (swelling)
- **Hypertonic**: Solution with higher solute outside; water leaves cell (shrinking)
- **Aquaporin**: Transmembrane water channel; facilitates rapid osmosis
- **Primary Active Transport**: Direct ATP hydrolysis; powers ion pumps
- **Secondary Active Transport**: Coupled to ion gradients; symport and antiport
- **Na+/K+ ATPase**: Pumps 3 Na+ out and 2 K+ in; maintains resting potential
- **Calcium Pump**: SERCA and PMCA types; regulates calcium signaling
- **Proton Pump**: H+ ATPase; acidifies organelles
- **Phagocytosis**: Cell eating; engulfs solid particles
- **Pinocytosis**: Cell drinking; engulfs fluids
- **Receptor-Mediated Endocytosis**: Clathrin-coated pits; specific ligand uptake

### Cellular Processes
- **Cellular Respiration**: Breakdown of glucose to produce ATP
- **Fermentation**: Anaerobic ATP production
- **Mitosis**: Cell division producing two identical diploid cells
- **Meiosis**: Cell division producing four non-identical haploid gametes
- **Endocytosis**: Cell membrane engulfing material to bring it inside
- **Exocytosis**: Vesicle fusion with membrane to release contents

#### Cellular Respiration
- **Glycolysis**: Glucose to pyruvate in cytoplasm; net 2 ATP, 2 NADH
- **Energy Investment Phase**: First half of glycolysis; consumes 2 ATP
- **Energy Payoff Phase**: Second half of glycolysis; produces 4 ATP and 2 NADH
- **Acetyl-CoA Formation**: Pyruvate oxidation; links glycolysis to Krebs cycle
- **Krebs Cycle**: Citric acid cycle; produces NADH, FADH₂, CO₂ in mitochondrial matrix
- **Electron Transport Chain**: Oxidative phosphorylation; produces ~32-34 ATP per glucose
- **Complex I**: NADH dehydrogenase; pumps 4 H+ across membrane
- **Complex II**: Succinate dehydrogenase; feeds electrons from FADH₂
- **Complex III**: Cytochrome bc₁ complex; pumps 4 H+ across membrane
- **Complex IV**: Cytochrome c oxidase; reduces O₂ to H₂O, pumps 2 H+
- **Chemiosmosis**: H+ gradient drives ATP synthesis through ATP synthase
- **Lactic Acid Fermentation**: Pyruvate to lactate; regenerates NAD+ in muscle cells
- **Alcoholic Fermentation**: Pyruvate to ethanol + CO₂; occurs in yeast

#### Photosynthesis
- **Light Reactions**: Convert light to chemical energy (ATP and NADPH) in thylakoids
- **Photosystem II**: P680 reaction center; oxidizes water, generates O₂
- **Photosystem I**: P700 reaction center; reduces NADP+ to NADPH
- **Photolysis**: Water splitting; source of electrons and O₂
- **Thylakoid Electron Transport**: Plastoquinone, cytochrome b₆f; generates proton gradient
- **Calvin Cycle**: Carbon fixation in stroma; uses ATP and NADPH to make glucose
- **RuBisCO**: Enzyme catalyzing CO₂ + RuBP → 3-PGA; carbon fixation
- **Reduction Phase**: 3-PGA to G3P; consumes ATP and NADPH
- **Regeneration Phase**: G3P to RuBP; continues the cycle

#### Cell Signaling
- **G Protein-Coupled Receptor**: Seven transmembrane domains; activates G proteins
- **Receptor Tyrosine Kinase**: Enzyme-linked receptor; phosphorylates tyrosine residues
- **Ion Channel Receptor**: Ligand-gated channel; rapid electrical signaling
- **Intracellular Receptor**: Nuclear or cytoplasmic; binds lipid-soluble signals
- **G Protein Activation**: GDP to GTP exchange; alpha subunit dissociates
- **cAMP Pathway**: Adenylyl cyclase activation; activates protein kinase A
- **Phospholipase C Pathway**: IP₃ and DAG production; Ca²⁺ release and PKC activation
- **Receptor Dimerization**: Ligand binding causes pairing; cross-phosphorylation
- **Ras-MAPK Pathway**: GTPase signaling cascade; regulates gene expression
- **PI3K-Akt Pathway**: Cell survival and growth; metabolic regulation
- **Second Messenger**: Amplifies signals; includes cAMP, Ca²⁺, IP₃, DAG
- **Cyclic AMP**: From ATP by adenylyl cyclase; activates PKA
- **IP₃**: Inositol triphosphate; triggers Ca²⁺ release from ER
- **DAG**: Diacylglycerol; remains in membrane, activates protein kinase C
- **Calcium Signaling**: Ca²⁺ ions as messengers; regulates many processes
- **Calmodulin**: Calcium-binding protein; activates various enzymes
- **MAPK Cascade**: Raf → MEK → ERK; controls proliferation
- **Phosphorylation**: Addition of phosphate groups; changes protein activity

### Genetic Processes
- **DNA Replication**: Copying genetic material before cell division
- **RNA Processing**: Modification of pre-mRNA (splicing, capping, polyadenylation)
- **Protein Synthesis**: Translation of genetic code into functional proteins
- **Crossing Over**: Exchange of genetic material between homologous chromosomes

### Evolutionary Processes
- **Mutation**: Source of genetic variation
- **Selection**: Differential survival and reproduction
- **Drift**: Random changes in allele frequencies
- **Migration**: Movement of individuals between populations

### Ecological Processes
- **Primary Production**: Conversion of light/chemical energy into organic compounds
- **Decomposition**: Breakdown of organic matter by decomposers
- **Predation**: Consumption of one organism by another
- **Competition**: Organisms competing for limited resources
- **Mutualism**: Symbiotic relationship benefiting both species
- **Parasitism**: Relationship where one organism benefits at the expense of another

## Genetics

### DNA Structure and Replication
- **Double Helix**: Watson-Crick model; two antiparallel strands; right-handed spiral
- **Nucleotide**: Building block; phosphate + sugar (deoxyribose) + nitrogenous base
- **Purines**: Adenine (A) and Guanine (G); two-ring structure
- **Pyrimidines**: Cytosine (C) and Thymine (T); single-ring structure
- **Base Pairing**: A-T (2 hydrogen bonds); G-C (3 hydrogen bonds); complementary
- **Antiparallel**: Strands run in opposite directions; 5' to 3' and 3' to 5'
- **Major Groove**: Wide groove; site for protein binding
- **Minor Groove**: Narrow groove; protein recognition
- **Helicase**: Unwinds DNA helix; breaks hydrogen bonds
- **Single-Strand Binding Proteins**: Stabilize unwound DNA; prevent reannealing
- **Primase**: Synthesizes RNA primers; provides 3'-OH for DNA polymerase
- **DNA Polymerase III**: Main replication enzyme in prokaryotes; adds nucleotides 5' to 3'
- **DNA Polymerase I**: Removes primers; fills gaps in prokaryotes
- **DNA Polymerase δ and ε**: Eukaryotic replication enzymes
- **Leading Strand**: Continuous synthesis; toward replication fork
- **Lagging Strand**: Discontinuous synthesis; away from fork; Okazaki fragments
- **Okazaki Fragments**: Short DNA segments on lagging strand; 100-200 nt in eukaryotes
- **DNA Ligase**: Seals nicks; joins Okazaki fragments
- **Topoisomerase**: Relieves tension; cuts and rejoins DNA ahead of replication fork
- **Telomeres**: Repetitive sequences at chromosome ends; TTAGGG in humans
- **Telomerase**: Enzyme adding telomere repeats; active in germ cells and cancer cells
- **Semiconservative Replication**: Each daughter DNA has one old and one new strand
- **Replication Fork**: Y-shaped structure; site of DNA synthesis
- **Origin of Replication**: Starting point; oriC in prokaryotes; multiple in eukaryotes
- **Proofreading**: DNA polymerase 3' to 5' exonuclease; error correction
- **Mismatch Repair**: Post-replication error correction; recognizes distortions

### Transcription
- **RNA Polymerase**: Synthesizes RNA from DNA template; does not require primer
- **Promoter**: DNA sequence where RNA polymerase binds; upstream of gene
- **TATA Box**: Eukaryotic promoter element; ~25 bp upstream of start site
- **Transcription Factors**: Proteins assisting RNA polymerase binding; TFIID, TFIIB, etc.
- **Initiation**: RNA polymerase binds promoter; DNA unwinds; transcription begins
- **Elongation**: RNA polymerase moves along DNA; adds ribonucleotides 5' to 3'
- **Termination**: RNA polymerase releases; transcript completed
- **Template Strand**: DNA strand read 3' to 5'; antisense strand
- **Coding Strand**: Non-template strand; same sequence as RNA (except T→U); sense strand
- **Transcription Bubble**: Unwound DNA region during transcription; ~8-9 bp
- **Rho-Independent Termination**: Hairpin structure in RNA; causes release in prokaryotes
- **Rho-Dependent Termination**: Rho protein; terminates transcription in prokaryotes

### RNA Processing (Eukaryotes)
- **Pre-mRNA**: Primary transcript; contains introns and exons
- **5' Cap**: 7-methylguanosine; added to 5' end; protects from degradation; aids translation
- **3' Poly-A Tail**: ~200 adenines; added to 3' end; stability and translation
- **Splicing**: Removal of introns; joining of exons
- **Spliceosome**: Complex of snRNPs and proteins; catalyzes splicing
- **snRNA**: Small nuclear RNA; U1, U2, U4, U5, U6; components of spliceosome
- **Intron**: Non-coding sequence; removed during splicing
- **Exon**: Coding sequence; retained in mature mRNA
- **Alternative Splicing**: Different combinations of exons; multiple proteins from one gene
- **Self-Splicing Introns**: Group I and II introns; catalytic RNA (ribozymes)

### Translation
- **Ribosome**: Protein synthesis machinery; large and small subunits
- **80S Ribosome**: Eukaryotic; 60S + 40S subunits
- **A Site**: Aminoacyl-tRNA binding site; accepts incoming tRNA
- **P Site**: Peptidyl-tRNA site; holds tRNA with growing chain
- **E Site**: Exit site; tRNA leaves ribosome
- **tRNA**: Transfer RNA; adaptor molecule; anticodon and amino acid attachment site
- **Anticodon**: Three nucleotides on tRNA; complementary to mRNA codon
- **Aminoacyl-tRNA Synthetase**: Attaches amino acid to correct tRNA; ensures fidelity
- **Codon**: Three-nucleotide sequence; codes for amino acid
- **Start Codon**: AUG; codes for methionine; translation initiation
- **Stop Codons**: UAA, UAG, UGA; no corresponding tRNA; translation termination
- **Reading Frame**: Grouping of nucleotides in triplets; determines protein sequence
- **Wobble Base Pairing**: Relaxed pairing at third codon position; allows tRNA flexibility
- **Polysome**: Multiple ribosomes translating single mRNA; efficient protein production
- **Kozak Sequence**: Eukaryotic consensus around AUG; affects translation efficiency
- **Shine-Dalgarno Sequence**: Prokaryotic ribosome binding site; upstream of AUG
- **Initiation Complex**: Ribosome + mRNA + initiator tRNA; begins translation
- **Elongation Factors**: EF-Tu, EF-G in prokaryotes; EF-1, EF-2 in eukaryotes
- **Peptidyl Transferase**: Ribosomal RNA enzyme; catalyzes peptide bond formation
- **Release Factors**: Recognize stop codons; terminate translation
- **Signal Peptide**: N-terminal sequence; directs protein to ER
- **Signal Recognition Particle (SRP)**: Binds signal peptide; pauses translation; targets ribosome to ER

### Genetic Code
- **Triplet Code**: Three nucleotides = one amino acid
- **Degeneracy**: Multiple codons for same amino acid; reduces mutation impact
- **Universality**: Same code in nearly all organisms; evidence for common ancestry
- **Non-Overlapping**: Each nucleotide part of only one codon
- **64 Codons**: 61 amino acid codons + 3 stop codons
- **20 Amino Acids**: Standard set in proteins

### Gene Regulation - Prokaryotes
- **Operon**: Cluster of genes under single promoter; coordinately regulated
- **lac Operon**: Lactose metabolism genes; negative and positive regulation
- **Repressor**: Protein blocking transcription; binds operator
- **Operator**: DNA sequence; repressor binding site
- **Inducer**: Molecule inactivating repressor; allolactose for lac operon
- **CAP-cAMP**: Catabolite activator protein; positive regulation; enhances transcription
- **trp Operon**: Tryptophan synthesis genes; repressible system
- **Corepressor**: Molecule activating repressor; tryptophan for trp operon
- **Attenuation**: Premature termination; ribosome-mediated in trp operon

### Gene Regulation - Eukaryotes
- **Chromatin Remodeling**: Changes in DNA-histone interactions; affects accessibility
- **Histone Acetylation**: Addition of acetyl groups; loosens chromatin; activates transcription
- **Histone Deacetylation**: Removal of acetyl groups; condenses chromatin; represses transcription
- **DNA Methylation**: Addition of methyl to cytosine; gene silencing; epigenetic mark
- **CpG Islands**: Cytosine-guanine rich regions; often at promoters; methylation regulates genes
- **Enhancers**: Regulatory sequences; increase transcription; can be far from promoter
- **Silencers**: Regulatory sequences; decrease transcription
- **Transcription Factor Binding Sites**: DNA sequences; bind activators or repressors
- **Mediator Complex**: Bridge between transcription factors and RNA polymerase II
- **Combinatorial Control**: Multiple transcription factors; fine-tune gene expression
- **X-Inactivation**: Random silencing of one X chromosome in females; Barr body
- **Xist RNA**: Long non-coding RNA; coats inactive X; triggers silencing
- **Genomic Imprinting**: Parent-specific gene expression; methylation-based

### Mutations
- **Point Mutation**: Single nucleotide change
- **Silent Mutation**: No amino acid change; wobble position
- **Missense Mutation**: Different amino acid; may affect function
- **Nonsense Mutation**: Creates stop codon; truncated protein
- **Frameshift Mutation**: Insertion or deletion; not multiple of 3; shifts reading frame
- **Insertion**: Addition of nucleotides
- **Deletion**: Removal of nucleotides
- **Duplication**: Segment copied; gene dosage changes
- **Inversion**: Segment reversed; may disrupt genes
- **Translocation**: Segment moved to different chromosome; chromosomal rearrangement
- **Spontaneous Mutation**: Natural errors; DNA replication mistakes; ~10⁻⁹ per base per cell division
- **Induced Mutation**: Caused by mutagens; chemicals, radiation
- **Transition**: Purine to purine or pyrimidine to pyrimidine
- **Transversion**: Purine to pyrimidine or vice versa
- **Mutagen**: Agent causing mutations; UV, X-rays, chemicals
- **Ames Test**: Assay for mutagenicity; uses bacteria; predicts carcinogenicity

### Mendelian Genetics
- **Dominance**: Allele expressed in heterozygote
- **Recessive**: Allele masked by dominant; expressed only in homozygote
- **Homozygous**: Two identical alleles; AA or aa
- **Heterozygous**: Two different alleles; Aa
- **Law of Segregation**: Allele pairs separate during gamete formation
- **Law of Independent Assortment**: Genes on different chromosomes assort independently
- **Punnett Square**: Grid predicting offspring genotypes and phenotypes
- **Monohybrid Cross**: Single trait; 3:1 phenotypic ratio in F2
- **Dihybrid Cross**: Two traits; 9:3:3:1 ratio in F2 if independent
- **Test Cross**: Cross with homozygous recessive; reveals unknown genotype
- **Incomplete Dominance**: Heterozygote intermediate phenotype; pink flowers from red × white
- **Codominance**: Both alleles fully expressed; AB blood type
- **Multiple Alleles**: More than two alleles for gene; ABO blood types
- **Pleiotropy**: One gene affects multiple traits; sickle cell affects many systems
- **Epistasis**: One gene masks another; coat color in mice
- **Polygenic Inheritance**: Multiple genes contribute to one trait; height, skin color
- **Linked Genes**: On same chromosome; do not assort independently
- **Recombination Frequency**: Percent recombinant offspring; proportional to distance
- **Map Unit (centimorgan)**: 1% recombination = 1 map unit
- **Sex-Linked Traits**: Genes on X or Y chromosome; X-linked more common
- **X-Linked Recessive**: Hemophilia, color blindness; males more affected
- **Pedigree Analysis**: Family tree showing trait inheritance

### Chromosomes and Cell Division
- **Chromosome**: DNA-protein complex; carries genes
- **Sister Chromatids**: Identical copies; joined at centromere; formed during S phase
- **Centromere**: Constriction point; kinetochore attachment site
- **Kinetochore**: Protein structure; attaches spindle fibers to centromere
- **Telomere**: Chromosome ends; protect coding sequences
- **Homologous Chromosomes**: Paired chromosomes; one from each parent; same genes, possibly different alleles
- **Diploid (2n)**: Two sets of chromosomes; somatic cells
- **Haploid (n)**: One set of chromosomes; gametes
- **Karyotype**: Organized display of chromosomes; arranged by size and banding
- **Autosome**: Non-sex chromosome; 22 pairs in humans
- **Sex Chromosome**: X or Y; determines biological sex

### Mitosis
- **Interphase**: G1 (growth), S (DNA replication), G2 (preparation); not part of mitosis proper
- **Prophase**: Chromatin condenses; centrioles move to poles; nuclear envelope breaks down
- **Metaphase**: Chromosomes align at metaphase plate; spindle checkpoint
- **Anaphase**: Sister chromatids separate; move to opposite poles
- **Telophase**: Nuclear envelopes reform; chromosomes decondense; cleavage furrow begins
- **Cytokinesis**: Cytoplasm divides; contractile ring in animals; cell plate in plants
- **Mitotic Spindle**: Microtubule apparatus; separates chromosomes
- **Spindle Checkpoint**: Ensures proper chromosome attachment before anaphase
- **Cyclins**: Regulatory proteins; levels fluctuate during cell cycle
- **Cyclin-Dependent Kinases (CDKs)**: Enzymes activated by cyclins; drive cell cycle
- **G1/S Checkpoint**: Checks cell size, nutrients, DNA damage; restriction point
- **G2/M Checkpoint**: Ensures DNA replication complete and undamaged
- **p53**: Tumor suppressor; arrests cycle or triggers apoptosis if DNA damaged

### Meiosis
- **Meiosis I**: Reductional division; separates homologous chromosomes; produces haploid cells
- **Meiosis II**: Equational division; separates sister chromatids; like mitosis
- **Prophase I**: Longest phase; homologous pairing (synapsis); crossing over
- **Synapsis**: Pairing of homologous chromosomes; forms bivalents (tetrads)
- **Bivalent (Tetrad)**: Paired homologous chromosomes; four chromatids
- **Crossing Over**: Exchange of DNA between non-sister chromatids; genetic recombination
- **Chiasmata**: Points where crossing over occurred; visible in late prophase I
- **Metaphase I**: Bivalents align at metaphase plate; independent assortment
- **Anaphase I**: Homologous chromosomes separate; sister chromatids stay together
- **Telophase I**: Two haploid cells; chromosomes still duplicated
- **Prophase II**: Chromosomes condense; spindles form
- **Metaphase II**: Chromosomes align at metaphase plate
- **Anaphase II**: Sister chromatids separate
- **Telophase II**: Four haploid cells; genetically unique
- **Independent Assortment**: Random orientation of bivalents; 2ⁿ combinations (n = haploid number)
- **Genetic Variation**: Crossing over + independent assortment; produces unique gametes
- **Nondisjunction**: Failure of chromosomes to separate; causes aneuploidy
- **Aneuploidy**: Abnormal chromosome number; Down syndrome (trisomy 21)
- **Monosomy**: Missing one chromosome; 2n-1; usually lethal except Turner syndrome (XO)
- **Trisomy**: Extra chromosome; 2n+1; Down (21), Edwards (18), Patau (13)

## Evolution

### Mechanisms of Evolution
- **Variation**: Differences among individuals; raw material for evolution
- **Heritable Variation**: Genetic differences passed to offspring; basis for selection
- **Overproduction**: More offspring than survive; competition for resources
- **Differential Survival**: Some variants survive/reproduce better
- **Adaptation**: Trait improving survival/reproduction in specific environment

### Natural Selection Types
- **Directional Selection**: Favors one extreme; shifts population mean; antibiotic resistance
- **Stabilizing Selection**: Favors intermediate; reduces variation; human birth weight
- **Disruptive Selection**: Favors both extremes; can lead to speciation; beak sizes
- **Sexual Selection**: Traits increasing mating success; peacock tails, deer antlers
- **Artificial Selection**: Human-directed breeding; domestication

### Population Genetics
- **Gene Pool**: All alleles in population
- **Allele Frequency**: Proportion of specific allele; p + q = 1 for two alleles
- **Hardy-Weinberg Equation**: p² + 2pq + q² = 1; predicts genotype frequencies
- **Hardy-Weinberg Conditions**: Large population, random mating, no mutation, no migration, no selection
- **Deviation from H-W**: Indicates evolution occurring

### Genetic Drift
- **Random Fluctuation**: Chance events change allele frequencies
- **Founder Effect**: New population from few individuals; reduced variation
- **Bottleneck Effect**: Population crash; survivors not representative; reduced diversity
- **Greater Impact in Small Populations**: Drift stronger when N small

### Gene Flow (Migration)
- **Immigration**: Individuals entering population; introduces alleles
- **Emigration**: Individuals leaving; removes alleles
- **Homogenizing Effect**: Reduces differences between populations
- **Counteracts Local Adaptation**: Maladaptive alleles introduced

### Mutation
- **Ultimate Source**: Only creates new alleles
- **Usually Deleterious**: Most mutations harmful or neutral
- **Low Rate**: ~10⁻⁸ to 10⁻⁹ per base per generation
- **Provides Raw Material**: Selection acts on mutation-generated variation

### Speciation
- **Biological Species Concept**: Interbreeding populations producing fertile offspring; reproductively isolated from others
- **Reproductive Isolation**: Barriers preventing gene flow between species
- **Prezygotic Barriers**: Prevent fertilization
  - **Habitat Isolation**: Different habitats; rarely encounter
  - **Temporal Isolation**: Breed at different times
  - **Behavioral Isolation**: Different courtship behaviors
  - **Mechanical Isolation**: Anatomical incompatibility
  - **Gametic Isolation**: Sperm-egg incompatibility
- **Postzygotic Barriers**: Reduce hybrid viability/fertility
  - **Hybrid Inviability**: Hybrid dies before reproductive maturity
  - **Hybrid Sterility**: Hybrid survives but sterile; mule (horse × donkey)
  - **Hybrid Breakdown**: F1 fertile, F2 or later generations sterile/weak
- **Allopatric Speciation**: Geographic isolation; populations separated by barrier
- **Sympatric Speciation**: Within same area; no physical barrier; polyploidy in plants
- **Polyploidy**: Multiple chromosome sets; autopolyploid (same species) or allopolyploid (different species)
- **Adaptive Radiation**: Rapid diversification into many niches; Darwin's finches

### Evidence for Evolution
- **Fossil Record**: Chronological history; shows change over time; transitional forms
- **Comparative Anatomy**: Similarities reveal common ancestry
  - **Homologous Structures**: Similar structure, different function; vertebrate limbs
  - **Analogous Structures**: Similar function, different structure; bird/insect wings; convergent evolution
  - **Vestigial Structures**: Reduced/functionless remnants; human appendix, whale pelvis
- **Embryology**: Developmental similarities; all vertebrate embryos have pharyngeal pouches, tail
- **Molecular Biology**: DNA/protein sequence similarities; molecular clock
- **Biogeography**: Species distribution patterns; island species resemble mainland ancestors
- **Direct Observation**: Microevolution observed; bacteria, insects, plants

### Phylogeny and Systematics
- **Phylogenetic Tree**: Diagram of evolutionary relationships; branching pattern
- **Node**: Branching point; represents common ancestor
- **Branch**: Lineage; connects nodes
- **Root**: Common ancestor of all taxa in tree
- **Tip (Leaf)**: Terminal taxa; living or extinct species
- **Sister Taxa**: Share immediate common ancestor
- **Monophyletic (Clade)**: Ancestor and all descendants; natural group
- **Paraphyletic**: Ancestor and some descendants; excludes some (e.g., reptiles without birds)
- **Polyphyletic**: Distantly related taxa grouped by convergence; not evolutionary group
- **Outgroup**: Taxon outside group of interest; helps root tree
- **Derived Character (Synapomorphy)**: Shared trait evolved in common ancestor
- **Ancestral Character (Plesiomorphy)**: Trait present in ancestor; retained
- **Parsimony**: Simplest explanation; fewest evolutionary changes
- **Maximum Likelihood**: Statistical method; most probable tree given data
- **Molecular Clock**: Constant mutation rate; estimates divergence time
- **Horizontal Gene Transfer**: Genes transferred between species; common in prokaryotes

## Ecology

### Population Ecology
- **Density**: Individuals per unit area or volume
- **Dispersion Patterns**: Clumped (resources patchy), uniform (territoriality), random (rare)
- **Survivorship Curves**: 
  - **Type I**: High survival until old age; large mammals, humans
  - **Type II**: Constant mortality; birds, rodents
  - **Type III**: High early mortality; fish, plants, invertebrates
- **Life Table**: Age-specific survival and reproduction data
- **Cohort**: Group born at same time; followed through life

### Population Growth
- **Exponential Growth**: J-shaped curve; dN/dt = rN; unlimited resources
- **r (Intrinsic Rate)**: Per capita growth rate; birth rate - death rate
- **Logistic Growth**: S-shaped curve; dN/dt = rN(1 - N/K); limited resources
- **K (Carrying Capacity)**: Maximum sustainable population size
- **Density-Dependent Factors**: Effects intensify with density; competition, predation, disease
- **Density-Independent Factors**: Effects regardless of density; weather, natural disasters
- **r-Selected Species**: High reproduction, low parental care, short life; weeds, insects
- **K-Selected Species**: Low reproduction, high parental care, long life; elephants, whales

### Community Ecology
- **Species Richness**: Number of species
- **Species Evenness**: Relative abundance of species
- **Diversity Index**: Combines richness and evenness; Shannon, Simpson indices
- **Dominant Species**: Most abundant or biomass; strong community effects
- **Keystone Species**: Disproportionate effect relative to abundance; sea otters, wolves
- **Foundation Species**: Creates habitat for others; trees, corals

### Interspecific Interactions
- **Competition**: Both species harmed; resource limitation
  - **Competitive Exclusion**: Superior competitor eliminates other; Gause's principle
  - **Resource Partitioning**: Species use different resources/times; reduces competition
  - **Character Displacement**: Divergent evolution where species overlap
- **Predation**: Predator benefits, prey harmed
  - **Coevolution**: Reciprocal evolutionary change; predator-prey arms race
  - **Cryptic Coloration**: Camouflage; reduces predation
  - **Aposematic Coloration**: Warning colors; bright colors signal toxicity
  - **Batesian Mimicry**: Harmless mimics toxic; monarch butterfly
  - **Müllerian Mimicry**: Multiple toxic species resemble each other; shared warning
- **Herbivory**: Herbivore benefits, plant harmed
  - **Plant Defenses**: Thorns, toxins, tough leaves
  - **Induced Defenses**: Produced after damage; tannins, protease inhibitors
- **Symbiosis**: Close long-term interaction
  - **Mutualism**: Both benefit; nitrogen-fixing bacteria, pollinators
  - **Commensalism**: One benefits, other unaffected; barnacles on whale
  - **Parasitism**: Parasite benefits, host harmed; tapeworms, ticks

### Community Succession
- **Primary Succession**: Starts on barren substrate; no soil; after volcano, glacier retreat
- **Pioneer Species**: First colonizers; lichens, mosses; create soil
- **Secondary Succession**: Disturbed but soil remains; after fire, agriculture abandonment
- **Climax Community**: Stable endpoint; self-perpetuating; oak-hickory forest
- **Facilitation**: Early species make environment suitable for later species
- **Inhibition**: Early species prevent establishment of others
- **Tolerance**: Later species tolerate conditions; outcompete early species

### Energy Flow and Nutrient Cycling
- **Primary Productivity**: Rate of energy capture by producers
  - **Gross Primary Productivity (GPP)**: Total photosynthesis
  - **Net Primary Productivity (NPP)**: GPP - respiration; available to consumers
- **Secondary Productivity**: Consumer biomass production
- **Energy Pyramid**: Energy decreases at higher trophic levels; ~10% transfer efficiency
- **Biomass Pyramid**: Typically decreases with trophic level
- **10% Rule**: ~10% energy transferred to next trophic level; rest lost as heat

### Biogeochemical Cycles
- **Water Cycle**: Evaporation, transpiration, precipitation, runoff
- **Carbon Cycle**: Photosynthesis, respiration, decomposition, combustion, ocean uptake
- **Nitrogen Cycle**:
  - **Nitrogen Fixation**: N₂ to NH₃; bacteria (Rhizobium), lightning
  - **Nitrification**: NH₃ → NO₂⁻ → NO₃⁻; nitrifying bacteria
  - **Assimilation**: Plants uptake NO₃⁻; incorporate into organic molecules
  - **Ammonification**: Decomposers convert organic N to NH₃
  - **Denitrification**: NO₃⁻ → N₂; returns nitrogen to atmosphere
- **Phosphorus Cycle**: No atmospheric component; weathering, uptake, decomposition
- **Limiting Nutrient**: Scarce nutrient restricting productivity; often N or P

### Biomes
- **Tropical Rainforest**: High rainfall, temperature; highest biodiversity; year-round growing season
- **Tropical Dry Forest**: Pronounced dry season; deciduous trees
- **Savanna**: Grassland with scattered trees; seasonal rainfall; fire-adapted
- **Desert**: Very low precipitation (<30 cm/yr); extreme temperatures; CAM plants
- **Chaparral**: Mediterranean climate; hot dry summers, mild wet winters; fire-adapted shrubs
- **Temperate Grassland**: Moderate rainfall; cold winters, hot summers; deep fertile soil
- **Temperate Broadleaf Forest**: Four distinct seasons; deciduous trees; moderate rainfall
- **Northern Coniferous Forest (Taiga)**: Long cold winters; short summers; evergreen conifers
- **Tundra**: Permafrost; very cold; short growing season; low diversity; dwarf plants
- **Climate Diagram**: Temperature and precipitation over year; predicts biome type

### Aquatic Ecosystems
- **Freshwater**: Lakes, streams, rivers, wetlands
  - **Photic Zone**: Light penetrates; photosynthesis occurs
  - **Aphotic Zone**: No light; decomposers and detritivores
  - **Oligotrophic Lake**: Nutrient-poor; clear water; deep
  - **Eutrophic Lake**: Nutrient-rich; murky; shallow; high productivity
  - **Littoral Zone**: Shallow water; rooted plants
  - **Limnetic Zone**: Open water; phytoplankton
  - **Benthic Zone**: Bottom; sediments; detritivores
- **Marine**: Oceans, estuaries, coral reefs
  - **Intertidal Zone**: Between high and low tide; extreme variation
  - **Neritic Zone**: Shallow water over continental shelf; high productivity
  - **Oceanic Zone**: Open ocean; low nutrient availability
  - **Pelagic Zone**: Open water column
  - **Abyssal Zone**: Deep ocean floor; cold, dark, high pressure
  - **Hydrothermal Vents**: Chemosynthetic bacteria; unique ecosystems
  - **Coral Reefs**: High diversity; symbiotic zooxanthellae; warm shallow water
  - **Estuary**: Freshwater-saltwater mixing; high productivity; nursery grounds

### Conservation Biology
- **Biodiversity Hotspots**: High species richness and endemism; threatened
- **Endangered Species**: Risk of extinction; IUCN Red List categories
- **Habitat Fragmentation**: Breaks continuous habitat; reduces population size; edge effects
- **Edge Effects**: Altered conditions at habitat boundaries; increased predation, invasives
- **Corridor**: Habitat strips connecting fragments; facilitates movement
- **Invasive Species**: Non-native; causes ecological/economic harm; outcompetes natives
- **Overexploitation**: Unsustainable harvesting; fishing, hunting
- **Climate Change**: Shifts ranges, phenology; alters ecosystems; coral bleaching
- **Extinction Vortex**: Small population size; inbreeding, drift; further decline
- **Minimum Viable Population**: Smallest size for long-term persistence


## Organisms

### Plant Structure and Function
- **Root System**: Anchors plant; absorbs water and minerals; stores nutrients
  - **Taproot**: Single main root; deep penetration; dicots
  - **Fibrous Roots**: Many thin roots; shallow; monocots
  - **Root Hairs**: Extensions of epidermal cells; increase surface area
  - **Root Cap**: Protects apical meristem; gravitropism sensing
  - **Endodermis**: Innermost cortex layer; Casparian strip controls water entry to vascular cylinder
  - **Casparian Strip**: Waxy barrier in endodermis; forces selective uptake
- **Shoot System**: Stems, leaves, flowers; photosynthesis and reproduction
  - **Node**: Point where leaves attach
  - **Internode**: Stem segment between nodes
  - **Apical Bud**: Terminal bud; primary growth
  - **Axillary Bud**: Lateral bud; side branches
  - **Apical Dominance**: Terminal bud inhibits lateral buds; auxin-mediated
- **Xylem**: Water and mineral transport; dead cells at maturity
  - **Tracheids**: Elongated cells; pits for water passage; all vascular plants
  - **Vessel Elements**: Wider, shorter; perforation plates; angiosperms only
  - **Lignin**: Strengthens cell walls; provides support
- **Phloem**: Sugar transport; living cells
  - **Sieve Tube Elements**: Conducting cells; lack nucleus at maturity
  - **Companion Cells**: Support sieve tubes; metabolic functions
  - **Sieve Plates**: Perforated end walls; allow cytoplasmic continuity
- **Meristems**: Regions of cell division; indeterminate growth
  - **Apical Meristems**: Tips of roots and shoots; primary growth (length)
  - **Lateral Meristems**: Vascular cambium and cork cambium; secondary growth (width)
- **Tissues**:
  - **Dermal Tissue**: Outer protective layer; epidermis
  - **Ground Tissue**: Between dermal and vascular; parenchyma, collenchyma, sclerenchyma
  - **Vascular Tissue**: Xylem and phloem; transport

### Plant Transport
- **Transpiration**: Water evaporation from leaves; creates tension pulling water up
- **Cohesion-Tension Theory**: Water molecules stick together; tension pulls water column
- **Stomata**: Pores in epidermis; gas exchange; bordered by guard cells
- **Guard Cells**: Control stomatal opening; K⁺ influx causes swelling and opening
- **Transpiration-Cohesion-Tension Mechanism**: Driving force for water ascent
- **Root Pressure**: Push from below; osmotic uptake; contributes to water rise
- **Guttation**: Water droplets exuded from leaves; due to root pressure
- **Pressure-Flow Hypothesis**: Sugar transport mechanism; source to sink
  - **Source**: Sugar-producing tissue; leaf during day
  - **Sink**: Sugar-consuming tissue; roots, fruits, growing regions
  - **Loading**: Active transport of sugars into phloem at source
  - **Unloading**: Removal of sugars from phloem at sink

### Plant Reproduction
- **Alternation of Generations**: Multicellular diploid (sporophyte) and haploid (gametophyte) stages
- **Sporophyte**: Diploid; produces spores by meiosis; dominant in vascular plants
- **Gametophyte**: Haploid; produces gametes by mitosis; dominant in bryophytes
- **Flower Parts**:
  - **Sepals**: Outermost; protective; collectively calyx
  - **Petals**: Colorful; attract pollinators; collectively corolla
  - **Stamens**: Male reproductive organs; anther + filament
  - **Anther**: Produces pollen (male gametophytes)
  - **Carpel (Pistil)**: Female reproductive organ; stigma + style + ovary
  - **Stigma**: Receives pollen
  - **Style**: Connects stigma to ovary
  - **Ovary**: Contains ovules (female gametophytes)
- **Pollination**: Transfer of pollen to stigma; wind, insects, birds, bats
- **Double Fertilization**: Unique to angiosperms
  - **Sperm 1 + Egg → Zygote (2n) → Embryo**
  - **Sperm 2 + Two Polar Nuclei → Endosperm (3n) → Food storage**
- **Seed**: Embryo + stored food + seed coat; develops from ovule
- **Fruit**: Mature ovary; protects and disperses seeds; only in angiosperms
- **Seed Dispersal**: Wind, water, animals; reduces competition

### Plant Hormones
- **Auxin**: Cell elongation; apical dominance; phototropism; gravitropism
- **Cytokinin**: Cell division; delays senescence; opposes auxin in lateral bud growth
- **Gibberellin**: Stem elongation; seed germination; flowering
- **Abscisic Acid (ABA)**: Stomatal closure; seed dormancy; stress response
- **Ethylene**: Fruit ripening; leaf abscission; response to stress
- **Phototropism**: Growth toward light; auxin accumulates on shaded side
- **Gravitropism**: Growth response to gravity; roots positive, shoots negative
- **Thigmotropism**: Growth response to touch; climbing vines

### Animal Organ Systems
- **Digestive System**: Ingestion, digestion, absorption, elimination
  - **Mouth**: Mechanical breakdown; saliva with amylase (starch digestion)
  - **Esophagus**: Transports food to stomach; peristalsis
  - **Stomach**: Acidic (pH 2); pepsin (protein digestion); churning
  - **Small Intestine**: Major digestion and absorption; villi increase surface area
    - **Duodenum**: First section; receives bile and pancreatic enzymes
    - **Jejunum**: Middle; main absorption site
    - **Ileum**: Final section; absorbs bile salts and vitamin B12
  - **Liver**: Produces bile; detoxifies; stores glycogen
  - **Gallbladder**: Stores and concentrates bile
  - **Pancreas**: Produces digestive enzymes; bicarbonate neutralizes stomach acid
  - **Large Intestine (Colon)**: Water reabsorption; feces formation; bacteria
- **Circulatory System**: Transport nutrients, gases, wastes, hormones
  - **Heart**: Muscular pump; four chambers in mammals/birds
  - **Atria**: Receiving chambers; thin walls
  - **Ventricles**: Pumping chambers; thick walls
  - **Arteries**: Carry blood away from heart; thick elastic walls; high pressure
  - **Veins**: Return blood to heart; thin walls; valves prevent backflow
  - **Capillaries**: Thin-walled; site of exchange between blood and tissues
  - **Systemic Circuit**: Heart → body → heart; oxygenated blood to tissues
  - **Pulmonary Circuit**: Heart → lungs → heart; deoxygenation then oxygenation
  - **Double Circulation**: Mammal/bird; separate pulmonary and systemic circuits
- **Respiratory System**: Gas exchange; obtain O₂, release CO₂
  - **Lungs**: Main organ; mammals; millions of alveoli
  - **Alveoli**: Tiny air sacs; thin walls; surrounded by capillaries; gas exchange surface
  - **Diaphragm**: Muscle; contracts to expand thoracic cavity; inhalation
  - **Trachea**: Windpipe; cartilage rings prevent collapse
  - **Bronchi**: Branch from trachea into each lung
  - **Bronchioles**: Smaller branches; lead to alveoli
  - **Hemoglobin**: Protein in red blood cells; binds O₂; contains iron
- **Excretory System**: Remove metabolic wastes; maintain water/salt balance
  - **Kidneys**: Filter blood; produce urine; regulate osmolarity
  - **Nephron**: Functional unit of kidney; ~1 million per kidney
  - **Glomerulus**: Capillary ball; filtration of blood plasma
  - **Bowman's Capsule**: Surrounds glomerulus; collects filtrate
  - **Proximal Tubule**: Reabsorbs glucose, amino acids, ions, water
  - **Loop of Henle**: Creates osmotic gradient; concentrates urine
  - **Distal Tubule**: Further reabsorption; pH regulation
  - **Collecting Duct**: Final water reabsorption; ADH regulation
  - **Ureter**: Transports urine to bladder
  - **Bladder**: Stores urine
  - **Urethra**: Urine exits body
- **Nervous System**: Rapid communication; electrical and chemical signals
  - **Central Nervous System (CNS)**: Brain and spinal cord
  - **Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)**: Nerves outside CNS
  - **Somatic**: Voluntary control; skeletal muscles
  - **Autonomic**: Involuntary; smooth muscle, cardiac muscle, glands
    - **Sympathetic**: Fight or flight; increases heart rate, inhibits digestion
    - **Parasympathetic**: Rest and digest; decreases heart rate, stimulates digestion
  - **Neuron**: Nerve cell; dendrites, cell body, axon, synaptic terminals
  - **Action Potential**: Electrical signal; all-or-none; propagates along axon
  - **Synapse**: Junction between neurons; neurotransmitter release
  - **Neurotransmitter**: Chemical messenger; acetylcholine, dopamine, serotonin, etc.
- **Endocrine System**: Slow, long-lasting communication; hormones in blood
  - **Hypothalamus**: Links nervous and endocrine; produces releasing hormones
  - **Pituitary**: Master gland; anterior (hormones) and posterior (stores hormones from hypothalamus)
  - **Thyroid**: Thyroxine; metabolism regulation; calcitonin (lowers blood Ca²⁺)
  - **Parathyroid**: Parathyroid hormone (PTH); raises blood Ca²⁺
  - **Adrenal**: Medulla (epinephrine/norepinephrine); cortex (cortisol, aldosterone)
  - **Pancreas**: Insulin (lowers blood glucose); glucagon (raises blood glucose)
  - **Gonads**: Ovaries (estrogen, progesterone); testes (testosterone)
  - **Negative Feedback**: Most common; output inhibits further secretion
  - **Positive Feedback**: Rare; output stimulates more secretion; childbirth (oxytocin)
- **Immune System**: Defense against pathogens
  - **Innate Immunity**: Nonspecific; rapid; barriers, phagocytes, inflammation, complement
  - **Adaptive Immunity**: Specific; slower; memory; B cells and T cells
  - **B Cells**: Produce antibodies; humoral immunity
  - **T Cells**: Cell-mediated immunity; helper T cells coordinate; cytotoxic T cells kill infected cells
  - **Antibodies**: Y-shaped proteins; bind antigens; mark for destruction
  - **Antigen**: Foreign molecule triggering immune response
  - **Memory Cells**: Long-lived; rapid response upon reinfection
  - **Vaccination**: Introduces weakened/dead pathogen; establishes memory

### Animal Reproduction
- **Asexual**: Single parent; clones; budding, fragmentation, parthenogenesis
- **Sexual**: Two parents; genetic variation; meiosis and fertilization
- **External Fertilization**: Eggs and sperm released into water; fish, amphibians
- **Internal Fertilization**: Sperm deposited in female; reptiles, birds, mammals
- **Oviparous**: Egg-laying; birds, most reptiles
- **Viviparous**: Live birth; most mammals
- **Ovoviviparous**: Eggs retained inside mother; some sharks, snakes
- **Gametogenesis**: Gamete production
  - **Spermatogenesis**: Sperm production; continuous after puberty; four sperm per meiosis
  - **Oogenesis**: Egg production; one egg per meiosis; others become polar bodies
- **Menstrual Cycle**: ~28 days in humans
  - **Follicular Phase**: Follicle development; estrogen rises
  - **Ovulation**: Egg release; day 14
  - **Luteal Phase**: Corpus luteum produces progesterone; prepares uterus
  - **Menstruation**: If no implantation; uterine lining shed
- **Pregnancy**: Gestation period; embryo/fetus develops in uterus
  - **Placenta**: Exchange organ; nutrients and O₂ in, wastes and CO₂ out
  - **Umbilical Cord**: Connects fetus to placenta
  - **Trimesters**: Three 3-month periods
  - **Labor**: Contractions; oxytocin positive feedback; delivery

## Diversity of Life

### Three Domains
- **Bacteria**: Prokaryotes; peptidoglycan cell walls; diverse metabolisms
- **Archaea**: Prokaryotes; distinct from bacteria; extremophiles; unique lipids
- **Eukarya**: Eukaryotes; membrane-bound organelles; nucleus

### Bacteria (Domain)
- **Shapes**: Cocci (spherical), bacilli (rod), spirilla (spiral)
- **Gram Staining**: Differentiates cell wall structure
- **Metabolic Diversity**: Photoautotrophs, chemoautotrophs, heterotrophs
- **Nitrogen Fixation**: Convert N₂ to NH₃; symbiotic (Rhizobium) or free-living
- **Decomposers**: Recycle nutrients; essential for ecosystems
- **Pathogenic**: Disease-causing; Streptococcus, Salmonella, Mycobacterium
- **Endospores**: Dormant resistant structures; survive extreme conditions
- **Conjugation**: Horizontal gene transfer; plasmid exchange via pilus
- **Transformation**: Uptake of naked DNA from environment
- **Transduction**: Viral-mediated gene transfer
- **Cyanobacteria**: Photosynthetic; oxygenic; produced Earth's O₂

### Archaea (Domain)
- **Methanogens**: Produce methane; anaerobic; swamps, guts
- **Halophiles**: Salt-loving; Great Salt Lake, Dead Sea
- **Thermophiles**: Heat-loving; hot springs, hydrothermal vents
- **Acidophiles**: Acid-loving; pH < 2
- **Unique Features**: Ether-linked lipids; no peptidoglycan; histones present

### Protists (Kingdom)
- **Paraphyletic Group**: Diverse eukaryotes; mostly unicellular
- **Algae**: Photosynthetic protists
  - **Diatoms**: Silica shells; major phytoplankton
  - **Dinoflagellates**: Two flagella; some bioluminescent; red tides
  - **Green Algae**: Closest relatives to plants; Chlorophyta
  - **Red Algae**: Phycoerythrin pigment; deep water; Rhodophyta
  - **Brown Algae**: Large seaweeds; kelp forests; Phaeophyta
- **Protozoa**: Heterotrophic protists
  - **Amoebozoans**: Pseudopodia for movement; Amoeba
  - **Ciliates**: Cilia for movement; Paramecium; two nuclei
  - **Flagellates**: Flagella for movement; Trypanosoma (sleeping sickness), Giardia
  - **Apicomplexans**: Parasitic; Plasmodium (malaria)
- **Slime Molds**: Feed as amoebas; aggregate for reproduction
  - **Plasmodial**: Multinucleate mass; single large cell
  - **Cellular**: Individual amoebas aggregate

### Fungi (Kingdom)
- **Heterotrophic**: Absorptive nutrition; secrete enzymes externally
- **Hyphae**: Filamentous structures; collectively form mycelium
- **Mycelium**: Network of hyphae; feeding structure
- **Chitin**: Cell wall material; same as arthropod exoskeleton
- **Saprotrophic**: Decompose dead organic matter
- **Parasitic**: Feed on living hosts; athlete's foot, rusts, smuts
- **Mutualistic**: Mycorrhizae (with plant roots); lichens (with algae/cyanobacteria)
- **Mycorrhizae**: Fungus-root association; increase nutrient uptake
  - **Ectomycorrhizae**: Surround root; don't penetrate cells
  - **Arbuscular Mycorrhizae**: Penetrate root cells; arbuscules
- **Lichen**: Fungus + photosynthetic partner; indicator species (air quality)
- **Major Groups**:
  - **Chytrids**: Aquatic; flagellated spores; most primitive
  - **Zygomycetes**: Bread molds; zygosporangium
  - **Ascomycetes**: Sac fungi; yeasts, morels, truffles; ascospores
  - **Basidiomycetes**: Club fungi; mushrooms, puffballs; basidiospores
- **Reproduction**: Asexual (spores) and sexual (fusion of hyphae)

### Plants (Kingdom Plantae)
- **Embryophytes**: Embryo develops within parent tissue
- **Alternation of Generations**: Sporophyte and gametophyte stages
- **Bryophytes**: Nonvascular; gametophyte dominant; mosses, liverworts, hornworts
  - **No True Roots**: Rhizoids for anchorage
  - **Require Water**: For sperm to swim to egg
  - **Small Size**: Limited by lack of vascular tissue
- **Seedless Vascular Plants**: Xylem and phloem; sporophyte dominant; ferns, horsetails, club mosses
  - **Larger Size**: Vascular tissue supports height
  - **Still Require Water**: For fertilization
- **Gymnosperms**: Naked seeds; no fruit; conifers, cycads, ginkgo
  - **Conifers**: Pines, spruces, firs; needle leaves; cones
  - **Pollen**: Male gametophyte; wind-dispersed; no water needed for fertilization
  - **Seeds**: Dispersal and protection; dormancy
- **Angiosperms**: Flowering plants; enclosed seeds in fruits; dominant land plants
  - **Flowers**: Reproductive structures; attract pollinators
  - **Fruits**: Mature ovary; seed dispersal
  - **Double Fertilization**: Unique feature; endosperm nutrition
  - **Monocots**: One cotyledon; parallel veins; scattered vascular bundles; flower parts in 3s
  - **Dicots (Eudicots)**: Two cotyledons; netted veins; ring vascular bundles; flower parts in 4s or 5s

### Animals (Kingdom Animalia)
- **Heterotrophic**: Consume other organisms
- **Multicellular**: Specialized tissues and organs
- **Motile**: At some life stage
- **Sexual Reproduction**: Dominant; gamete formation by meiosis
- **Development**: Blastula stage; gastrulation

### Animal Phyla - Major Groups
- **Porifera (Sponges)**: No true tissues; filter feeders; choanocytes; sessile
- **Cnidaria**: Radial symmetry; diploblastic; two body forms (polyp, medusa)
  - **Hydra, Jellyfish, Corals, Sea Anemones**
  - **Cnidocytes**: Stinging cells with nematocysts
  - **Gastrovascular Cavity**: Single opening; digestion and circulation
- **Platyhelminthes (Flatworms)**: Bilateral symmetry; triploblastic; acoelomate
  - **Planarians, Flukes, Tapeworms**
  - **Cephalization**: Head with sensory structures
  - **No Circulatory/Respiratory Systems**: Flat for diffusion
- **Nematoda (Roundworms)**: Pseudocoelomate; complete digestive tract
  - **C. elegans**: Model organism
  - **Many Parasitic**: Hookworms, pinworms, heartworms
- **Mollusca**: Coelomate; soft body; often with shell
  - **Body Plan**: Muscular foot, visceral mass, mantle
  - **Classes**: Gastropods (snails, slugs), Bivalves (clams, mussels), Cephalopods (octopus, squid)
  - **Open Circulatory System**: Except cephalopods (closed)
- **Annelida (Segmented Worms)**: Metameric segmentation; closed circulatory system
  - **Earthworms, Leeches, Polychaetes**
  - **Setae**: Bristles for traction
- **Arthropoda**: Segmented body; exoskeleton; jointed appendages; most diverse phylum
  - **Exoskeleton**: Chitin; must molt (ecdysis) to grow
  - **Open Circulatory System**: Hemolymph
  - **Chelicerates**: Spiders, scorpions, ticks, horseshoe crabs; chelicerae; no antennae
  - **Crustaceans**: Crabs, lobsters, shrimp; two pairs antennae; mostly aquatic
  - **Insects**: Three body parts (head, thorax, abdomen); three pairs legs; one pair antennae; most have wings
  - **Myriapods**: Millipedes, centipedes; many legs
- **Echinodermata**: Deuterostomes; radial symmetry (adults); endoskeleton; water vascular system
  - **Sea Stars, Sea Urchins, Sea Cucumbers**
  - **Tube Feet**: Hydraulic locomotion and feeding
  - **Regeneration**: Can regrow lost parts
- **Chordata**: Notochord, dorsal hollow nerve cord, pharyngeal slits, post-anal tail (at some stage)
  - **Vertebrates**: Backbone replaces notochord; cranium; distinct head
    - **Fishes**: Jawless (lampreys), Cartilaginous (sharks, rays), Bony (most fishes)
    - **Amphibians**: Frogs, salamanders; moist skin; metamorphosis; aquatic larvae
    - **Reptiles**: Amniotic egg; scales; ectothermic; lizards, snakes, turtles, crocodiles
    - **Birds**: Endothermic; feathers; lightweight bones; four-chambered heart; efficient respiration
    - **Mammals**: Endothermic; hair; mammary glands; three middle ear bones; diverse teeth
      - **Monotremes**: Egg-laying; platypus, echidnas
      - **Marsupials**: Pouched; kangaroos, opossums
      - **Eutherians (Placentals)**: Placenta; most mammals

### Evolutionary Innovations
- **Tissues**: Cnidaria onward
- **Bilateral Symmetry**: Flatworms onward
- **Coelom**: True body cavity; mollusks, annelids, arthropods, echinoderms, chordates
- **Segmentation**: Annelids, arthropods, chordates
- **Exoskeleton**: Arthropods
- **Endoskeleton**: Echinoderms, chordates
- **Notochord → Backbone**: Vertebrates
- **Jaws**: Gnathostomes (jawed vertebrates)
- **Limbs**: Tetrapods (amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals)
- **Amniotic Egg**: Reptiles, birds, mammals; allowed land colonization
- **Endothermy**: Birds, mammals; constant body temperature
