# Chemistry Equations Reference

## ‚öõÔ∏è Atomic Structure & Periodicity

### Atomic Mass and Isotopes
- **Average atomic mass**: $\text{Atomic mass} = \sum (\text{isotope mass} \times \text{fractional abundance})$
- **Mass number**: $A = Z + N$ where Z = protons, N = neutrons

### Electron Configuration
- **Energy of photon**: $E = hf = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$ where $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34}$ J¬∑s
- **Rydberg equation** (hydrogen): $\frac{1}{\lambda} = R_H\left(\frac{1}{n_1^2} - \frac{1}{n_2^2}\right)$ where $R_H = 1.097 \times 10^7$ m‚Åª¬π
- **Energy levels** (hydrogen): $E_n = -\frac{2.18 \times 10^{-18}}{n^2}$ J

### Periodic Trends
- **Effective nuclear charge**: $Z_{eff} = Z - S$ where S = shielding constant
- **Ionization energy**: Energy to remove electron (increases left to right, decreases top to bottom)
- **Electron affinity**: Energy change when adding electron (more negative = more favorable)
- **Atomic radius**: Decreases left to right, increases top to bottom

## üîó Chemical Bonding

### Lewis Structures
- **Octet rule**: Atoms tend to gain/lose/share electrons to achieve 8 valence electrons
- **Formal charge**: $FC = V - N - \frac{B}{2}$ where V = valence e‚Åª, N = non-bonding e‚Åª, B = bonding e‚Åª

### Bond Energy and Length
- **Bond order**: $\text{Bond order} = \frac{\text{bonding e‚Åª} - \text{antibonding e‚Åª}}{2}$
- **Bond enthalpy**: Energy required to break one mole of bonds
- Higher bond order ‚Üí shorter bond length ‚Üí stronger bond

### Electronegativity
- **Dipole moment**: $\mu = Q \times r$ (measured in Debye, D)
- **Percent ionic character**: $\% \text{ ionic} = \frac{\mu_{measured}}{\mu_{ionic}} \times 100\%$

### VSEPR Theory
- Electron domain geometry determines molecular shape
- Lone pairs repel more than bonding pairs

### Valence Bond Theory
- **Hybridization**:
  - sp: 2 electron domains (linear, 180¬∞)
  - sp¬≤: 3 electron domains (trigonal planar, 120¬∞)
  - sp¬≥: 4 electron domains (tetrahedral, 109.5¬∞)
  - sp¬≥d: 5 electron domains (trigonal bipyramidal)
  - sp¬≥d¬≤: 6 electron domains (octahedral, 90¬∞)

### Molecular Orbital Theory
- $\sigma$ bonds: head-on overlap
- $\pi$ bonds: side-by-side overlap
- Bond order = (bonding - antibonding)/2

## ‚öñÔ∏è Stoichiometry

### Mole Concept
- **Avogadro's number**: $N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23}$ particles/mol
- $\text{moles} = \frac{\text{mass (g)}}{\text{molar mass (g/mol)}}$
- $\text{molecules} = \text{moles} \times N_A$

### Percent Composition
- $\% \text{ by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of element}}{\text{total mass}} \times 100\%$

### Empirical and Molecular Formulas
- **Empirical formula**: Simplest whole number ratio
- **Molecular formula**: Actual number of atoms
- $\text{Molecular formula} = (\text{Empirical formula})_n$

### Limiting Reactant
- Calculate moles of product from each reactant
- The reactant producing least product is limiting
- **Theoretical yield**: Maximum product from limiting reactant
- **Percent yield**: $\% \text{ yield} = \frac{\text{actual yield}}{\text{theoretical yield}} \times 100\%$

### Solution Concentration
- **Molarity**: $M = \frac{\text{moles solute}}{\text{liters solution}}$
- **Molality**: $m = \frac{\text{moles solute}}{\text{kg solvent}}$
- **Mole fraction**: $\chi_A = \frac{n_A}{n_{total}}$
- **Mass percent**: $\% = \frac{\text{mass solute}}{\text{mass solution}} \times 100\%$
- **ppm**: $\text{ppm} = \frac{\text{mass solute}}{\text{mass solution}} \times 10^6$
- **Dilution**: $M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$

## üíß Solutions and Colligative Properties

### Raoult's Law
- $P_{solution} = \chi_{solvent} P¬∞_{solvent}$
- $\Delta P = \chi_{solute} P¬∞_{solvent}$

### Colligative Properties
- **Boiling point elevation**: $\Delta T_b = K_b \times m$ where $K_b$ = ebullioscopic constant
- **Freezing point depression**: $\Delta T_f = K_f \times m$ where $K_f$ = cryoscopic constant
- **Osmotic pressure**: $\Pi = MRT$ where R = 0.0821 L¬∑atm/(mol¬∑K)

### Van't Hoff Factor
- $i = \frac{\text{actual number of particles}}{\text{formula units dissolved}}$
- For colligative properties: multiply by i
- $\Delta T_f = i \times K_f \times m$
- $\Pi = iMRT$

## üå°Ô∏è Thermochemistry

### Enthalpy
- **Heat at constant pressure**: $\Delta H = q_p$
- **Calorimetry**: $q = mc\Delta T$
- $q_{rxn} = -q_{surroundings}$

### Hess's Law
- $\Delta H¬∞_{rxn} = \sum \Delta H¬∞_f(\text{products}) - \sum \Delta H¬∞_f(\text{reactants})$
- Enthalpy is a state function (path independent)

### Bond Enthalpies
- $\Delta H¬∞_{rxn} = \sum \text{bonds broken} - \sum \text{bonds formed}$
- Breaking bonds requires energy (endothermic)
- Forming bonds releases energy (exothermic)

### Entropy
- **Second law**: $\Delta S_{universe} = \Delta S_{system} + \Delta S_{surroundings} > 0$ (spontaneous)
- **Standard entropy change**: $\Delta S¬∞ = \sum S¬∞(\text{products}) - \sum S¬∞(\text{reactants})$
- **Entropy change (isothermal)**: $\Delta S = \frac{q_{rev}}{T}$

### Gibbs Free Energy
- $\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$
- $\Delta G < 0$: spontaneous
- $\Delta G = 0$: equilibrium
- $\Delta G > 0$: non-spontaneous
- **Standard free energy**: $\Delta G¬∞ = \sum \Delta G¬∞_f(\text{products}) - \sum \Delta G¬∞_f(\text{reactants})$
- **Relationship to K**: $\Delta G¬∞ = -RT\ln K$
- **Non-standard conditions**: $\Delta G = \Delta G¬∞ + RT\ln Q$

## üí® Gases

### Ideal Gas Law
- $PV = nRT$ where $R = 0.0821$ L¬∑atm/(mol¬∑K) or $8.314$ J/(mol¬∑K)
- **Combined gas law**: $\frac{P_1V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2V_2}{T_2}$

### Individual Gas Laws
- **Boyle's law**: $P_1V_1 = P_2V_2$ (constant T, n)
- **Charles's law**: $\frac{V_1}{T_1} = \frac{V_2}{T_2}$ (constant P, n)
- **Avogadro's law**: $\frac{V_1}{n_1} = \frac{V_2}{n_2}$ (constant P, T)
- **Gay-Lussac's law**: $\frac{P_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2}{T_2}$ (constant V, n)

### Dalton's Law
- $P_{total} = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 + ...$
- $P_i = \chi_i P_{total}$ where $\chi_i$ = mole fraction

### Kinetic Molecular Theory
- **Average kinetic energy**: $\langle KE \rangle = \frac{3}{2}RT$ per mole
- **Root mean square speed**: $u_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}}$ where M = molar mass in kg/mol
- **Graham's law** (effusion/diffusion): $\frac{r_1}{r_2} = \sqrt{\frac{M_2}{M_1}}$

### Real Gases
- **van der Waals equation**: $\left(P + \frac{an^2}{V^2}\right)(V - nb) = nRT$
- a = accounts for intermolecular attractions
- b = accounts for molecular volume

## ‚ö° Chemical Kinetics

### Rate Laws
- **Rate** = $-\frac{1}{a}\frac{\Delta[A]}{\Delta t} = -\frac{1}{b}\frac{\Delta[B]}{\Delta t} = \frac{1}{c}\frac{\Delta[C]}{\Delta t}$
- **Rate law**: $\text{Rate} = k[A]^m[B]^n$
- **Overall order**: $m + n$
- k = rate constant (units depend on order)

### Integrated Rate Laws

**Zero order**: $[A]_0 - [A]_t = kt$
- Half-life: $t_{1/2} = \frac{[A]_0}{2k}$
- Linear plot: [A] vs t

**First order**: $\ln[A]_t - \ln[A]_0 = -kt$ or $\ln\frac{[A]_t}{[A]_0} = -kt$
- Half-life: $t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{k}$ (constant, independent of concentration)
- Linear plot: ln[A] vs t

**Second order**: $\frac{1}{[A]_t} - \frac{1}{[A]_0} = kt$
- Half-life: $t_{1/2} = \frac{1}{k[A]_0}$
- Linear plot: 1/[A] vs t

### Arrhenius Equation
- $k = Ae^{-E_a/RT}$
- $\ln k = \ln A - \frac{E_a}{RT}$
- **Two-point form**: $\ln\frac{k_2}{k_1} = \frac{E_a}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{1}{T_2}\right)$
- A = frequency factor
- $E_a$ = activation energy

### Reaction Mechanisms
- **Elementary step rate**: Rate = k[reactants]
- **Rate-determining step**: Slowest step determines overall rate
- Catalyst lowers $E_a$, increases k, no effect on K or $\Delta G$

## ‚öñÔ∏è Chemical Equilibrium

### Equilibrium Constant
- $aA + bB \rightleftharpoons cC + dD$
- $K_c = \frac{[C]^c[D]^d}{[A]^a[B]^b}$ (concentrations)
- $K_p = \frac{P_C^c P_D^d}{P_A^a P_B^b}$ (partial pressures)
- $K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n}$ where $\Delta n = \text{moles gas products} - \text{moles gas reactants}$

### Reaction Quotient
- $Q = \frac{[\text{products}]}{[\text{reactants}]}$ (same form as K, but non-equilibrium concentrations)
- $Q < K$: reaction proceeds forward
- $Q = K$: at equilibrium
- $Q > K$: reaction proceeds reverse

### Le Ch√¢telier's Principle
- System at equilibrium responds to stress by shifting to minimize stress
- **Add reactant/product**: shifts away
- **Remove reactant/product**: shifts toward
- **Increase temperature**:
  - Endothermic ($\Delta H > 0$): shifts right (K increases)
  - Exothermic ($\Delta H < 0$): shifts left (K decreases)
- **Increase pressure/decrease volume**: shifts toward fewer moles of gas
- **Add catalyst**: No shift, reaches equilibrium faster

### Relationship to Thermodynamics
- $\Delta G¬∞ = -RT\ln K$
- $K > 1$: $\Delta G¬∞ < 0$ (products favored)
- $K < 1$: $\Delta G¬∞ > 0$ (reactants favored)
- $K = 1$: $\Delta G¬∞ = 0$ (neither favored)

## üß™ Acids and Bases

### pH and pOH
- $\text{pH} = -\log[H^+]$ or $[H^+] = 10^{-\text{pH}}$
- $\text{pOH} = -\log[OH^-]$ or $[OH^-] = 10^{-\text{pOH}}$
- $\text{pH} + \text{pOH} = 14.00$ at 25¬∞C
- $K_w = [H^+][OH^-] = 1.0 \times 10^{-14}$ at 25¬∞C
- $\text{p}K_w = 14.00$

### Acid-Base Strength
- **Acid dissociation constant**: $K_a = \frac{[H^+][A^-]}{[HA]}$
- **Base dissociation constant**: $K_b = \frac{[BH^+][OH^-]}{[B]}$
- **Conjugate pair relationship**: $K_a \times K_b = K_w$
- $\text{p}K_a = -\log K_a$ and $\text{p}K_b = -\log K_b$
- $\text{p}K_a + \text{p}K_b = 14.00$
- Stronger acid ‚Üí larger $K_a$, smaller $\text{p}K_a$

### pH Calculations

**Strong acids**: $[H^+] = [acid]$ (complete dissociation)
**Strong bases**: $[OH^-] = [base]$ (complete dissociation)

**Weak acids**:
- $K_a = \frac{x^2}{[HA]_0 - x} \approx \frac{x^2}{[HA]_0}$ if x << [HA]‚ÇÄ
- $\text{pH} = \frac{1}{2}(\text{p}K_a - \log[HA])$ (approximation)

**Weak bases**:
- $K_b = \frac{x^2}{[B]_0 - x} \approx \frac{x^2}{[B]_0}$ if x << [B]‚ÇÄ

### Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation
- $\text{pH} = \text{p}K_a + \log\frac{[A^-]}{[HA]}$ (buffer solution)
- $\text{pH} = \text{p}K_a$ when $[A^-] = [HA]$
- Buffer capacity maximum when pH = pK‚Çê

### Polyprotic Acids
- Multiple $K_a$ values: $K_{a1} > K_{a2} > K_{a3}$
- $\text{pH} \approx \text{p}K_a$ at each equivalence point

### Titration
- **Equivalence point**: moles acid = moles base (for 1:1 stoichiometry)
- $M_aV_a = M_bV_b$ (for 1:1)
- **Strong acid-strong base**: pH = 7 at equivalence point
- **Weak acid-strong base**: pH > 7 at equivalence point
- **Strong acid-weak base**: pH < 7 at equivalence point

## üßÇ Solubility Equilibria

### Solubility Product
- $K_{sp} = [A^+]^a[B^-]^b$ for $A_aB_b(s) \rightleftharpoons aA^+(aq) + bB^-(aq)$
- **Molar solubility (s)**:
  - $AB$: $K_{sp} = s^2$
  - $AB_2$: $K_{sp} = 4s^3$
  - $A_2B$: $K_{sp} = 4s^3$
  - $A_2B_3$: $K_{sp} = 108s^5$

### Precipitation
- **Ion product**: $Q = [A^+]^a[B^-]^b$
- $Q < K_{sp}$: no precipitate (unsaturated)
- $Q = K_{sp}$: at saturation
- $Q > K_{sp}$: precipitate forms

### Common Ion Effect
- Solubility decreases when common ion is added
- Le Ch√¢telier: shifts equilibrium toward solid

### Complex Ion Formation
- $K_f = \frac{[\text{complex}]}{[\text{metal ion}][\text{ligand}]^n}$
- Formation of complex increases solubility

## üîã Electrochemistry

### Oxidation-Reduction
- **Oxidation**: loss of electrons (increase in oxidation state)
- **Reduction**: gain of electrons (decrease in oxidation state)
- Reducing agent is oxidized; oxidizing agent is reduced

### Balancing Redox (Half-Reaction Method)
1. Split into half-reactions
2. Balance atoms (except O and H)
3. Balance O with H‚ÇÇO
4. Balance H with H‚Å∫ (acidic) or OH‚Åª (basic)
5. Balance charge with e‚Åª
6. Multiply to equalize e‚Åª
7. Add half-reactions

### Cell Potential
- $E¬∞_{cell} = E¬∞_{cathode} - E¬∞_{anode}$
- $E¬∞_{cell} = E¬∞_{reduction} - E¬∞_{oxidation}$
- **Standard reduction potential**: More positive = stronger oxidizing agent

### Nernst Equation
- $E_{cell} = E¬∞_{cell} - \frac{RT}{nF}\ln Q = E¬∞_{cell} - \frac{0.0592}{n}\log Q$ at 25¬∞C
- At equilibrium: $E_{cell} = 0$, $Q = K$
- $E¬∞_{cell} = \frac{0.0592}{n}\log K$ at 25¬∞C

### Free Energy and Cell Potential
- $\Delta G = -nFE_{cell}$
- $\Delta G¬∞ = -nFE¬∞_{cell}$
- F = 96,485 C/mol (Faraday's constant)
- n = moles of electrons transferred

### Electrolysis
- **Faraday's laws**: $\text{mass} = \frac{Q \times M}{nF} = \frac{I \times t \times M}{nF}$
- Q = charge (coulombs) = I √ó t
- I = current (amperes)
- t = time (seconds)
- M = molar mass
- n = electrons per mole

## ‚ò¢Ô∏è Nuclear Chemistry

### Nuclear Equations
- **Alpha decay**: $^A_ZX \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2}Y + ^4_2\alpha$
- **Beta minus**: $^A_ZX \rightarrow ^A_{Z+1}Y + ^0_{-1}\beta + \bar{\nu}_e$
- **Beta plus (positron)**: $^A_ZX \rightarrow ^A_{Z-1}Y + ^0_{+1}\beta + \nu_e$
- **Gamma emission**: $^A_ZX^* \rightarrow ^A_ZX + ^0_0\gamma$
- **Electron capture**: $^A_ZX + ^0_{-1}e \rightarrow ^A_{Z-1}Y + \nu_e$

### Radioactive Decay Kinetics
- $N_t = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$ (first-order kinetics)
- $\ln\frac{N_t}{N_0} = -\lambda t$
- **Half-life**: $t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda}$
- **Activity**: $A = \lambda N$ (disintegrations/time)
- Units: Becquerel (Bq) = 1 decay/s; Curie (Ci) = $3.7 \times 10^{10}$ Bq

### Mass-Energy Relationship
- $E = mc^2$ where c = $3.00 \times 10^8$ m/s
- **Binding energy**: $\Delta E = \Delta m \times c^2$
- 1 amu = 931.5 MeV/c¬≤
