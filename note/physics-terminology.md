# Physics Terminology

## Core Concepts from Physics Mindmaps

### Mechanics
- **Kinematics**: Study of motion without considering forces
- **Dynamics**: Study of motion and the forces causing it
- **Position**: Location of an object in space at a specific time
- **Displacement**: Change in position; vector from initial to final position
- **Velocity**: Rate of change of position; displacement per unit time
- **Speed**: Magnitude of velocity; distance per unit time
- **Acceleration**: Rate of change of velocity
- **Force**: Push or pull that can change an object's motion
- **Mass**: Measure of amount of matter in an object; resistance to acceleration
- **Weight**: Force of gravity on an object (W = mg)
- **Newton's Laws**: Three fundamental laws describing motion and forces
- **Inertia**: Tendency of object to resist changes in motion
- **Momentum**: Product of mass and velocity (p = mv)
- **Impulse**: Change in momentum; force applied over time
- **Energy**: Capacity to do work
- **Kinetic Energy**: Energy of motion (KE = ½mv²)
- **Potential Energy**: Stored energy due to position or configuration
- **Work**: Energy transferred by force over distance (W = Fd cos θ)
- **Power**: Rate of doing work or transferring energy
- **Conservation of Energy**: Total energy in isolated system remains constant
- **Conservation of Momentum**: Total momentum in isolated system remains constant
- **Friction**: Force opposing relative motion between surfaces
- **Normal Force**: Perpendicular force between surfaces in contact
- **Tension**: Pulling force transmitted through string, rope, or cable
- **Centripetal Force**: Force directed toward center causing circular motion
- **Centripetal Acceleration**: Acceleration toward center in circular motion

#### Kinematics
- **Distance vs Displacement**: Distance is scalar (always positive); displacement is vector
- **Average Velocity**: vₐᵥᵍ = Δx/Δt; total displacement over time interval
- **Instantaneous Velocity**: v = dx/dt; velocity at specific instant
- **Speed vs Velocity**: Speed is magnitude; velocity includes direction
- **Uniform Acceleration**: Constant acceleration; a = Δv/Δt
- **Kinematic Equations**: v = v₀ + at; x = x₀ + v₀t + ½at²; v² = v₀² + 2aΔx
- **Free Fall**: Motion under gravity only; a = g = 9.8 m/s²
- **Projectile Motion**: 2D motion under gravity; parabolic trajectory
- **Horizontal Motion**: Constant velocity; x = v₀ₓt
- **Vertical Motion**: Constant acceleration; y = v₀ᵧt - ½gt²
- **Range**: Horizontal distance traveled; R = v₀²sin(2θ)/g
- **Maximum Height**: Peak of trajectory; H = v₀ᵧ²/(2g)
- **Position-Time Graph**: Slope gives velocity; curved for accelerated motion
- **Velocity-Time Graph**: Slope gives acceleration; area gives displacement
- **Acceleration-Time Graph**: Area gives change in velocity

#### Newton's Laws
- **First Law (Inertia)**: Object maintains motion state without net force
- **Equilibrium Condition**: Net force = 0; at rest or constant velocity
- **Inertial Reference Frame**: Non-accelerating frame where Newton's laws valid
- **Second Law**: F = ma; quantifies force-acceleration relationship
- **Net Force**: Vector sum of all forces; Fₙₑₜ = ma
- **Mass-Acceleration Relation**: Inverse relationship; larger mass, smaller acceleration
- **Newton (Unit)**: 1 N = 1 kg·m/s²
- **Third Law**: Action-reaction pairs; equal magnitude, opposite direction
- **Action-Reaction Pairs**: Forces act on different objects
- **Static Friction**: Prevents motion; fₛ ≤ μₛfₙ
- **Kinetic Friction**: Opposes sliding; fₖ = μₖN
- **Coefficient of Friction**: Material property; μₛ > μₖ always
- **Rolling Friction**: Lower than sliding; wheels reduce friction
- **Centripetal Force**: Fᶜ = mv²/r; directed toward center
- **Centripetal Acceleration**: aᶜ = v²/r; perpendicular to velocity
- **Uniform Circular Motion**: Constant speed, changing velocity; period T = 2πr/v
- **Banked Curves**: Tilted for circular motion; reduces friction needed
- **Air Resistance**: Depends on speed and area; Fᴅ ∝ v²
- **Terminal Velocity**: Constant speed when drag equals weight

#### Energy and Work
- **KE Formula**: KE = ½mv²; scalar, always positive
- **KE Dependence**: Quadratic in velocity; doubling speed quadruples KE
- **Gravitational PE**: U = mgh; height relative to reference
- **Elastic PE**: U = ½kx²; spring compression or stretch
- **Reference Level**: Zero PE point is arbitrary; only changes matter
- **Mechanical Energy**: E = KE + PE; conserved without friction
- **Nonconservative Forces**: Friction, air resistance; convert to thermal energy
- **Energy Diagrams**: PE curves show force; equilibrium at PE minima
- **Isolated System**: No external forces; total energy constant
- **Work Definition**: W = Fd cosθ; force times displacement
- **Positive Work**: Force in direction of motion; increases KE
- **Negative Work**: Force opposite motion; decreases KE
- **Work-Energy Theorem**: Wₙₑₜ = ΔKE
- **Power Formula**: P = W/t = F·v; watt = joule per second
- **Average Power**: Total work over time; Pₐᵥᵍ = ΔE/Δt
- **Instantaneous Power**: P = F·v at specific instant

#### Rotational Motion
- **Angular Displacement**: Angle rotated; θ in radians
- **Angular Velocity**: ω = dθ/dt; rate of rotation in rad/s
- **Angular Acceleration**: α = dω/dt; rate of change of angular velocity
- **Angular Kinematic Equations**: ω = ω₀ + αt; similar to linear equations
- **Torque**: τ = rF sinθ = rF⊥; rotational force effect
- **Net Torque**: τₙₑₜ = Iα; rotational analog of F = ma
- **Lever Arm**: Perpendicular distance to axis; longer arm, greater torque
- **Rotational Equilibrium**: ∑τ = 0; no angular acceleration
- **Angular Momentum**: L = Iω for rigid body
- **Conservation of Angular Momentum**: L constant if net torque = 0
- **Angular Impulse**: τΔt = ΔL; torque-impulse theorem
- **Moment of Inertia**: I = ∑mr²; distribution of mass
- **Parallel Axis Theorem**: I = Iᶜₘ + Md²; shift rotation axis
- **Common Shape Formulas**: Disk: ½MR²; Sphere: ₂₅MR²; Rod: ₁₂₁₂ML²
- **Rolling Without Slipping**: v = ωr; no sliding at contact
- **Rolling KE**: KE = ½mv² + ½Iω²; translation plus rotation

#### Fluid Mechanics
- **Pressure**: P = F/A; acts in all directions
- **Pressure with Depth**: P = P₀ + ρgh; increases linearly
- **Pascal's Principle**: Pressure change transmitted equally; hydraulic systems
- **Atmospheric Pressure**: Pₐₜₘ = 101,325 Pa at sea level
- **Buoyancy**: Upward force on submerged object
- **Archimedes' Principle**: Fᵇ = ρᶠₗᵤᵢᵈVg; equals weight of displaced fluid
- **Floating**: Object floats if ρₒᵇⱼₑᶜ < ρᶠₗᵤᵢᵈ
- **Sinking**: Object sinks if ρₒᵇⱼₑᶜ > ρᶠₗᵤᵢᵈ
- **Apparent Weight**: Wₐₛₛₐᵣₑₙₜ = W - Fᵇᵤₒᵧₐₙₜ
- **Continuity Equation**: A₁v₁ = A₂v₂ for incompressible flow
- **Flow Rate**: Q = Av; volume per time
- **Incompressible Flow**: Density constant; volume flow rate conserved
- **Bernoulli's Equation**: P + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant
- **Dynamic Pressure**: ½ρv²; pressure due to motion
- **Static Pressure**: P term in Bernoulli; pressure in still fluid
- **Poiseuille's Law**: Flow rate through pipe; Q ∝ ΔPr⁴/L
- **Reynolds Number**: Re = ρvL/μ; predicts laminar vs turbulent
- **Viscosity**: Fluid resistance to flow; causes drag and energy loss

#### Gravitation
- **Newton's Law of Gravitation**: F = Gm₁m₂/r²; universal attractive force
- **Gravitational Constant**: G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ N·m²/kg²
- **Inverse Square Law**: Force decreases with distance squared
- **Gravitational Field**: g = GM/r²; force per unit mass
- **Acceleration Due to Gravity**: g = 9.8 m/s² at surface; varies with altitude
- **Field Superposition**: Total field = vector sum of multiple masses
- **Circular Orbit**: v = √(GM/r); centripetal force = gravity
- **Orbital Period**: T² ∝ r³; Kepler's third law
- **Escape Velocity**: vₑₛᶜₐₛₑ = √(2GM/r); minimum to escape gravity
- **Kepler's First Law**: Planets orbit in ellipses with Sun at one focus
- **Kepler's Second Law**: Equal areas in equal times; angular momentum conservation
- **Kepler's Third Law**: T²/r³ = constant for all planets around Sun
- **Gravitational Potential Energy**: U = -GMm/r; energy in gravitational field

### Waves & Oscillations
- **Wave**: Disturbance that transfers energy through space or matter
- **Oscillation**: Repetitive back-and-forth motion
- **Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)**: Periodic motion with restoring force proportional to displacement
- **Amplitude**: Maximum displacement from equilibrium
- **Frequency**: Number of oscillations or wave cycles per unit time
- **Period**: Time for one complete oscillation or wave cycle
- **Wavelength**: Distance between successive wave crests or troughs
- **Wave Speed**: Speed at which wave propagates through medium
- **Transverse Wave**: Wave where oscillation is perpendicular to propagation
- **Longitudinal Wave**: Wave where oscillation is parallel to propagation
- **Mechanical Wave**: Wave requiring medium for propagation
- **Electromagnetic Wave**: Wave that can propagate through vacuum
- **Sound Wave**: Longitudinal mechanical wave through medium
- **Wave Properties**: Characteristics like amplitude, frequency, wavelength
- **Interference**: Superposition of waves creating regions of enhancement/cancellation
- **Constructive Interference**: Waves combine to increase amplitude
- **Destructive Interference**: Waves combine to decrease amplitude
- **Standing Wave**: Wave pattern with stationary nodes and antinodes
- **Resonance**: System's large amplitude response at specific frequency
- **Doppler Effect**: Change in frequency due to relative motion
- **Beat**: Periodic variation in amplitude from superposition of close frequencies

### Thermodynamics
- **Temperature**: Measure of average kinetic energy of particles
- **Heat**: Energy transferred due to temperature difference
- **Thermal Equilibrium**: State where no net heat transfer occurs
- **Internal Energy**: Total kinetic and potential energy of particles in system
- **Thermodynamic System**: Region of space being studied
- **First Law of Thermodynamics**: Energy conservation (ΔU = Q - W)
- **Second Law of Thermodynamics**: Entropy of isolated system increases
- **Third Law of Thermodynamics**: Entropy approaches zero at absolute zero
- **Entropy**: Measure of disorder or randomness in system
- **Absolute Zero**: Lowest possible temperature (0 K = -273.15°C)
- **Heat Transfer**: Movement of thermal energy (conduction, convection, radiation)
- **Conduction**: Heat transfer through direct contact
- **Convection**: Heat transfer through fluid motion
- **Radiation**: Heat transfer through electromagnetic waves
- **Specific Heat**: Energy needed to raise temperature of unit mass by one degree
- **Latent Heat**: Energy for phase change without temperature change
- **Phase Change**: Transition between states of matter
- **Heat Engine**: Device converting thermal energy to mechanical work
- **Efficiency**: Ratio of useful output to input energy
- **Carnot Cycle**: Idealized thermodynamic cycle with maximum efficiency
- **Refrigerator**: Device transferring heat from cold to hot reservoir
- **Heat Pump**: Device transferring heat for heating purposes

### Electromagnetism
- **Electric Charge**: Fundamental property causing electromagnetic interactions
- **Electric Field**: Region where electric force is experienced
- **Electric Force**: Force between charged objects (Coulomb's law)
- **Electric Potential**: Electric potential energy per unit charge
- **Voltage**: Difference in electric potential between two points
- **Electric Current**: Flow of electric charge per unit time
- **Resistance**: Opposition to current flow in conductor
- **Ohm's Law**: Relationship V = IR between voltage, current, and resistance
- **Conductor**: Material allowing easy flow of electric charge
- **Insulator**: Material preventing flow of electric charge
- **Semiconductor**: Material with conductivity between conductor and insulator
- **Circuit**: Closed path for electric current flow
- **Series Circuit**: Circuit with single path for current
- **Parallel Circuit**: Circuit with multiple paths for current
- **Capacitor**: Device storing electric charge and energy
- **Capacitance**: Ability to store charge per unit voltage
- **Magnetic Field**: Region where magnetic force is experienced
- **Magnetic Force**: Force on moving charge or current in magnetic field
- **Magnetic Flux**: Measure of magnetic field passing through area
- **Electromagnetic Induction**: Generation of electric field from changing magnetic field
- **Faraday's Law**: Induced EMF proportional to rate of flux change
- **Lenz's Law**: Induced current opposes change causing it
- **Inductor**: Device storing energy in magnetic field
- **Inductance**: Property opposing change in current
- **Transformer**: Device changing AC voltage using induction
- **Electromagnetic Wave**: Self-propagating wave of electric and magnetic fields
- **Maxwell's Equations**: Four equations describing all of electromagnetism
- **Speed of Light**: Maximum speed in universe (c ≈ 3 × 10⁸ m/s)

### Optics
- **Light**: Electromagnetic radiation visible to human eye
- **Ray**: Straight-line path representing light propagation
- **Geometric Optics**: Study of light using ray model
- **Wave Optics**: Study of light using wave properties
- **Reflection**: Bouncing of light off surface
- **Law of Reflection**: Angle of incidence equals angle of reflection
- **Refraction**: Bending of light when entering different medium
- **Snell's Law**: Relates angles of incidence and refraction to refractive indices
- **Index of Refraction**: Ratio of speed of light in vacuum to speed in medium
- **Total Internal Reflection**: Complete reflection at boundary with less dense medium
- **Dispersion**: Separation of white light into colors
- **Mirror**: Smooth surface reflecting light
- **Lens**: Transparent object refracting light to form images
- **Focal Point**: Point where parallel rays converge after reflection/refraction
- **Focal Length**: Distance from optical center to focal point
- **Image**: Reproduction of object formed by optical system
- **Real Image**: Image formed where light rays actually converge
- **Virtual Image**: Image formed where light rays appear to originate
- **Magnification**: Ratio of image size to object size
- **Diffraction**: Bending of waves around obstacles
- **Interference**: Superposition of light waves
- **Coherence**: Constant phase relationship between waves
- **Polarization**: Restriction of light wave oscillations to specific direction
- **Double-Slit Experiment**: Demonstrates wave nature of light
- **Thin Film Interference**: Interference in light reflected from thin layers

## Electromagnetism

### Electric Charge and Coulomb's Law
- **Electric Charge (q)**: Property causing electromagnetic interaction; measured in coulombs (C)
- **Elementary Charge**: e = 1.602×10^{-19} C; proton/electron magnitude
- **Charge Conservation**: Total charge conserved in isolated system
- **Quantization**: q = ne; charge comes in discrete packets
- **Coulomb's Law**: F = k|q₁q₂|/r²; k = 8.99×10⁹ N·m²/C²
- **Permittivity of Free Space**: ε₀ = 8.85×10^{-12} C²/(N·m²); k = 1/(4πε₀)
- **Superposition**: Total force is vector sum of individual forces

### Electric Fields
- **Electric Field (E)**: Force per unit charge; E = F/q; vector; N/C or V/m
- **Field from Point Charge**: E = kq/r²; radial direction
- **Field Lines**: Tangent to E; density indicates strength; start on + end on -
- **Electric Dipole**: Two equal opposite charges separated by distance
  - **Dipole Moment**: p = qd; vector from - to +
  - **Torque**: τ = p × E = pE sin θ
  - **Potential Energy**: U = -p · E = -pE cos θ

### Electric Potential
- **Electric Potential (V)**: Potential energy per unit charge; scalar; volts (V) = J/C
- **Potential Difference**: ΔV = V_B - V_A = -∫_A^B E · dl
- **Point Charge Potential**: V = kq/r; zero at infinity
- **Relation to Field**: E = -∇V; field is negative gradient of potential
- **Equipotential Surfaces**: Constant V; perpendicular to field lines
- **Electron Volt (eV)**: Energy gained by electron through 1 V; 1 eV = 1.602×10^{-19} J

### Capacitance
- **Capacitor**: Stores charge/energy; two conductors
- **Capacitance (C)**: Q = CV; farads (F); depends on geometry/material
- **Parallel Plate**: C = ε₀A/d; A = area, d = separation
- **Energy Stored**: U = ½QV = ½CV² = Q²/(2C)
- **Dielectric**: Insulating material; increases C by factor κ (dielectric constant)
  - C = κC₀ = κε₀A/d where ε = κε₀
- **Series**: 1/C_eq = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + ...; charge same, voltages add
- **Parallel**: C_eq = C₁ + C₂ + ...; voltage same, charges add

### Electric Current
- **Current (I)**: Charge flow rate; I = dQ/dt; amperes (A) = C/s
- **Current Density**: J = I/A; A/(m²)
- **Drift Velocity**: Average velocity of charge carriers in conductor
- **Conventional Current**: Direction positive charges flow (opposite electrons)

### Resistance and Ohm's Law
- **Ohm's Law**: V = IR; R = resistance in ohms (Ω)
- **Resistance**: R = ρL/A; ρ = resistivity (Ω·m); L = length; A = cross-section
- **Conductivity**: σ = 1/ρ
- **Temperature Dependence**: R = R₀[1 + α(T - T₀)]; α = temperature coefficient
- **Power**: P = IV = I²R = V²/R; watts (W)

### DC Circuits
- **Series Resistors**: R_eq = R₁ + R₂ + ...; current same, voltages add
- **Parallel Resistors**: 1/R_eq = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + ...; voltage same, currents add
- **Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)**: Σ I_in = Σ I_out; charge conservation
- **Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)**: Σ ΔV = 0 around loop; energy conservation
- **RC Circuit**: Charging: Q(t) = Q₀(1 - e^{-t/(RC)}); Discharging: Q(t) = Q₀e^{-t/(RC)}
  - **Time Constant**: τ = RC; time to reach 63% of final value

### Magnetic Fields
- **Magnetic Field (B)**: Tesla (T) = Wb/m² = N/(A·m); or gauss (1 G = 10^{-4} T)
- **Magnetic Force on Moving Charge**: F = qv × B; magnitude F = qvB sin θ; perpendicular to v and B
- **Lorentz Force**: F = q(E + v × B); combined electric and magnetic
- **Cyclotron Motion**: Circular path; radius r = mv/(qB); period T = 2πm/(qB)
- **Magnetic Force on Current**: F = IL × B; magnitude F = ILB sin θ
- **Torque on Current Loop**: τ = NIABsin θ = μ × B; μ = NIA (magnetic moment)

### Sources of Magnetic Field
- **Biot-Savart Law**: dB = (μ₀/4π)(I dl × r̂)/r²
- **Permeability of Free Space**: μ₀ = 4π×10^{-7} T·m/A
- **Straight Wire**: B = μ₀I/(2πr); circular field lines
- **Circular Loop**: Center: B = μ₀I/(2R); Axis: B = μ₀IR²/[2(R²+x²)^{3/2}]
- **Solenoid**: B = μ₀nI; n = turns per length; uniform inside, zero outside
- **Toroid**: B = μ₀NI/(2πr); N = total turns
- **Ampère's Law**: ∮ B · dl = μ₀I_enclosed; for symmetric situations

### Electromagnetic Induction
- **Magnetic Flux**: Φ_B = ∫ B · dA; webers (Wb) = T·m²
- **Faraday's Law**: ε = -dΦ_B/dt; induced EMF opposes flux change
- **Lenz's Law**: Induced current opposes change causing it; conservation of energy
- **Motional EMF**: ε = BLv; conductor moving through field
- **Inductance (L)**: Φ_B = LI; henries (H); self-inductance
- **Solenoid Inductance**: L = μ₀n²Al; A = area, l = length
- **Energy in Inductor**: U = ½LI²
- **RL Circuit**: I(t) = (ε/R)(1 - e^{-t/(L/R)}); τ = L/R

### AC Circuits
- **Alternating Current**: I(t) = I₀ sin(ωt); V(t) = V₀ sin(ωt)
- **RMS Values**: I_rms = I₀/√2; V_rms = V₀/√2; for power calculations
- **Reactance**: Opposition to AC; depends on frequency
  - **Capacitive**: X_C = 1/(ωC); leads voltage by 90°
  - **Inductive**: X_L = ωL; lags voltage by 90°
- **Impedance**: Z = √(R² + (X_L - X_C)²); ohms; total opposition
- **Phase Angle**: tan φ = (X_L - X_C)/R
- **Resonance**: ω₀ = 1/√(LC); X_L = X_C; Z = R minimum
- **Power**: P_avg = I_rms V_rms cos φ; cos φ = power factor

### Maxwell's Equations
- **Gauss's Law**: ∮ E · dA = Q_enclosed/ε₀; electric flux from charge
- **Gauss's Law for Magnetism**: ∮ B · dA = 0; no magnetic monopoles
- **Faraday's Law**: ∮ E · dl = -dΦ_B/dt; changing B creates E
- **Ampère-Maxwell Law**: ∮ B · dl = μ₀(I + ε₀ dΦ_E/dt); current and changing E create B
- **Displacement Current**: I_d = ε₀ dΦ_E/dt; Maxwell's addition

### Electromagnetic Waves
- **Wave Speed**: c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3.00×10⁸ m/s in vacuum
- **Relation**: c = fλ; f = frequency, λ = wavelength
- **E and B Relationship**: E = cB; perpendicular; E × B gives direction
- **Intensity**: I = P/A = E₀B₀/(2μ₀) = E₀²/(2μ₀c); W/m²
- **Poynting Vector**: S = (E × B)/μ₀; energy flux; W/m²
- **Radiation Pressure**: p = I/c (absorbed); p = 2I/c (reflected)
- **Electromagnetic Spectrum**: Radio, microwave, IR, visible, UV, X-ray, gamma ray

## Modern Physics

### Special Relativity
- **Postulates**: 
  1. Laws same in all inertial frames
  2. Speed of light c constant for all observers
- **Relativity of Simultaneity**: Events simultaneous in one frame not in another
- **Time Dilation**: Δt = γΔt₀; moving clocks run slow
- **Length Contraction**: L = L₀/γ; moving objects shortened
- **Lorentz Factor**: γ = 1/√(1 - v²/c²) ≥ 1
- **Velocity Addition**: u = (u' + v)/(1 + u'v/c²); not Galilean u = u' + v
- **Relativistic Momentum**: p = γmv
- **Relativistic Energy**: E = γmc²
- **Rest Energy**: E₀ = mc²
- **Total Energy**: E² = (pc)² + (mc²)²
- **Kinetic Energy**: K = (γ - 1)mc² = E - mc²

### Photons and the Photoelectric Effect
- **Blackbody Radiation**: Planck: E = hf; h = 6.626×10^{-34} J·s
- **Photon**: Particle of light; E = hf = hc/λ
- **Photoelectric Effect**: Light ejects electrons from metal
  - **Threshold Frequency**: f₀; minimum for ejection; depends on material
  - **Work Function**: φ = hf₀; energy to free electron
  - **Einstein's Equation**: K_max = hf - φ
  - **Stopping Potential**: V_s; eV_s = K_max
- **Compton Effect**: X-ray scattering; photon loses energy to electron
  - **Wavelength Shift**: Δλ = (h/m_e c)(1 - cos θ); m_e = electron mass

### Wave-Particle Duality
- **de Broglie Wavelength**: λ = h/p = h/(mv); matter waves
- **Applies to All**: Electrons, atoms, molecules; most noticeable for small m or low v
- **Electron Diffraction**: Confirms wave nature; interference patterns

### Quantum Mechanics Basics
- **Wavefunction**: ψ(x,t); contains all information
- **Probability Density**: |ψ|²; probability per unit volume
- **Normalization**: ∫ |ψ|² dV = 1
- **Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle**: Δx Δp ≥ ℏ/2; ΔE Δt ≥ ℏ/2; ℏ = h/(2π)
- **Schrödinger Equation**: iℏ ∂ψ/∂t = Ĥψ; time-dependent
- **Time-Independent**: Ĥψ = Eψ; eigenvalue problem
- **Operators**: Observable quantities; Hermitian operators
- **Expectation Value**: ⟨A⟩ = ∫ ψ* Â ψ dV

### Atomic Structure
- **Rutherford Model**: Nucleus + orbiting electrons; unstable classically
- **Bohr Model**: Quantized orbits; only for hydrogen
  - **Angular Momentum**: L = nℏ; n = 1, 2, 3, ...
  - **Radius**: r_n = n²a₀; a₀ = 0.529 Å (Bohr radius)
  - **Energy**: E_n = -13.6 eV/n²; n = principal quantum number
  - **Spectral Lines**: ΔE = hf; transitions between levels
- **Quantum Numbers**:
  - n: Principal (1, 2, 3, ...); energy level
  - l: Orbital angular momentum (0 to n-1); shape (s, p, d, f)
  - m_l: Magnetic (-l to +l); orientation
  - m_s: Spin (±½); intrinsic angular momentum
- **Pauli Exclusion Principle**: No two electrons same four quantum numbers
- **Electron Configuration**: Fill lowest energy first; Aufbau, Hund's rules

### Atomic Spectra
- **Emission Spectrum**: Discrete wavelengths emitted; transitions down
- **Absorption Spectrum**: Dark lines in continuous spectrum; transitions up
- **Rydberg Formula**: 1/λ = R_H(1/n_f² - 1/n_i²); R_H = 1.097×10⁷ m^{-1}
- **Series**: Lyman (UV, n_f=1), Balmer (visible, n_f=2), Paschen (IR, n_f=3)

### X-Rays
- **Bremsstrahlung**: Continuous spectrum; deceleration radiation
- **Characteristic X-Rays**: Discrete lines; inner shell transitions
- **Moseley's Law**: √f = a(Z - b); frequency vs atomic number
- **X-Ray Diffraction**: Bragg's Law: 2d sin θ = nλ; crystal structure

### Nuclear Physics
- **Nucleus**: Protons (Z) + neutrons (N); mass number A = Z + N
- **Isotopes**: Same Z, different N
- **Strong Nuclear Force**: Short-range; binds nucleons; overcomes Coulomb repulsion
- **Mass Defect**: Δm = [Zm_p + Nm_n] - m_nucleus
- **Binding Energy**: BE = Δmc²; energy to disassemble
- **Binding Energy per Nucleon**: BE/A; maximum ~8.8 MeV at Fe-56
- **Radioactive Decay**: N(t) = N₀e^{-λt}; λ = decay constant
  - **Half-Life**: t₁/₂ = (ln 2)/λ = 0.693/λ
  - **Activity**: A = λN; decays per time; becquerel (Bq) = 1 decay/s
- **Alpha Decay**: ⁴₂He emission; A decreases by 4, Z by 2
- **Beta Decay**: Electron or positron emission; A same, Z changes by ±1
- **Gamma Decay**: Photon emission; A, Z unchanged; energy release
- **Nuclear Fission**: Heavy nucleus splits; neutrons + energy released
  - **Chain Reaction**: Neutrons cause more fissions
  - **Critical Mass**: Sustains chain reaction
- **Nuclear Fusion**: Light nuclei combine; enormous energy; requires high T, P

### Particle Physics
- **Elementary Particles**: Quarks, leptons, gauge bosons
- **Quarks**: Up, down, charm, strange, top, bottom; fractional charge; confined in hadrons
- **Leptons**: Electron, muon, tau, neutrinos; integer charge or neutral
- **Hadrons**: Made of quarks
  - **Baryons**: 3 quarks (proton, neutron)
  - **Mesons**: Quark-antiquark pair (pion, kaon)
- **Fundamental Forces**: Gravity, electromagnetic, weak, strong
- **Exchange Particles**: Photon (EM), W/Z bosons (weak), gluons (strong), graviton (gravity, hypothetical)
- **Antimatter**: Same mass, opposite charge; annihilates with matter
- **Conservation Laws**: Energy, momentum, charge, baryon number, lepton number


## Optics

### Geometric Optics
- **Ray Optics**: Light travels in straight lines (rays); valid when λ << object size
- **Law of Reflection**: θ_i = θ_r; angle of incidence = angle of reflection
- **Law of Refraction (Snell's Law)**: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
- **Index of Refraction**: n = c/v; v = speed in medium; n ≥ 1
- **Total Internal Reflection**: θ_i > θ_c where sin θ_c = n₂/n₁ (n₁ > n₂); no transmission
- **Dispersion**: n varies with λ; prism separates colors
- **Fermat's Principle**: Light takes path of least time

### Mirrors
- **Plane Mirror**: Virtual image; same size; di = -do (behind mirror)
- **Spherical Mirrors**: Curved reflecting surface
  - **Concave**: Curves inward; converging; f > 0
  - **Convex**: Curves outward; diverging; f < 0
- **Focal Point**: Parallel rays converge (or appear to); f = R/2
- **Mirror Equation**: 1/f = 1/do + 1/di; do = object distance; di = image distance
- **Magnification**: m = -di/do = hi/ho; negative = inverted
- **Real Image**: di > 0; light converges; can project on screen
- **Virtual Image**: di < 0; light appears to come from; cannot project

### Lenses
- **Thin Lens**: Refracts light; negligible thickness
  - **Converging (Convex)**: Thicker at center; f > 0; forms real/virtual images
  - **Diverging (Concave)**: Thinner at center; f < 0; forms virtual images only
- **Lens Equation**: 1/f = 1/do + 1/di; same form as mirror equation
- **Magnification**: m = -di/do = hi/ho
- **Lensmaker's Equation**: 1/f = (n-1)(1/R₁ - 1/R₂); R = radius of curvature
- **Power of Lens**: P = 1/f; diopters (D) = m⁻¹
- **Lens Combinations**: 1/f_eq = 1/f₁ + 1/f₂ (thin lenses in contact)

### Optical Instruments
- **Human Eye**: Cornea + lens focus on retina; accommodation changes f
  - **Near Point**: ~25 cm; closest clear focus
  - **Far Point**: Infinity for normal eye
  - **Myopia (Nearsightedness)**: Diverging lens corrects
  - **Hyperopia (Farsightedness)**: Converging lens corrects
- **Magnifying Glass**: Converging lens; angular magnification M = 25 cm/f
- **Microscope**: Two lenses; objective (short f) + eyepiece
  - **Total Magnification**: M = m_o × M_e
- **Telescope**: Two lenses; objective (long f) + eyepiece (short f)
  - **Angular Magnification**: M = -f_o/f_e
  - **Reflecting Telescope**: Uses mirror as objective

### Wave Optics
- **Huygens' Principle**: Each point on wavefront acts as source of secondary wavelets
- **Interference**: Superposition of waves
  - **Constructive**: Crest + crest; path difference = mλ
  - **Destructive**: Crest + trough; path difference = (m + ½)λ
- **Coherence**: Constant phase relationship; needed for stable interference

### Double-Slit Interference
- **Young's Experiment**: Two slits; interference pattern on screen
- **Bright Fringes**: d sin θ = mλ; m = 0, ±1, ±2, ...; d = slit separation
- **Dark Fringes**: d sin θ = (m + ½)λ
- **Fringe Spacing**: Δy = λL/d; L = screen distance
- **Central Maximum**: m = 0; brightest

### Single-Slit Diffraction
- **Pattern**: Central bright with dimmer secondary maxima
- **Dark Fringes**: a sin θ = mλ; m = ±1, ±2, ...; a = slit width
- **Central Maximum Width**: 2λL/a; wider for smaller a
- **Intensity**: I(θ) = I₀[sin(β)/β]²; β = (πa sin θ)/λ

### Diffraction Grating
- **Many Slits**: N slits; sharper, brighter maxima
- **Bright Fringes**: d sin θ = mλ; m = order
- **Resolving Power**: R = Nm; ability to separate wavelengths
- **Applications**: Spectroscopy; separate/analyze wavelengths

### Thin Film Interference
- **Reflection from Thin Film**: Oil, soap bubbles
- **Phase Change**: Reflection from higher n medium adds λ/2 shift
- **Constructive**: 2nt = mλ (no shift) or 2nt = (m + ½)λ (one shift)
- **Destructive**: 2nt = (m + ½)λ (no shift) or 2nt = mλ (one shift)
- **Colors**: Different λ constructively interfere at different angles

### Polarization
- **Polarized Light**: Electric field oscillates in one plane
- **Unpolarized**: Random orientations
- **Polarization Methods**: Selective absorption, reflection, scattering, birefringence
- **Malus's Law**: I = I₀ cos² θ; transmitted intensity through analyzer
- **Brewster's Angle**: tan θ_B = n₂/n₁; reflected light fully polarized

### Resolution and Diffraction Limit
- **Rayleigh Criterion**: θ_min = 1.22λ/D; D = aperture diameter
- **Minimum Resolvable Angle**: Two objects just resolved when central max of one overlaps first dark of other
- **Diffraction Limit**: Fundamental limit on optical resolution

## Thermodynamics

### Temperature and Heat
- **Temperature (T)**: Measure of average kinetic energy; Kelvin (K), Celsius (°C), Fahrenheit (°F)
- **Conversions**: T_K = T_C + 273.15; T_F = (9/5)T_C + 32
- **Absolute Zero**: 0 K = -273.15°C; minimum possible temperature
- **Thermal Equilibrium**: No net heat flow; same temperature
- **Zeroth Law**: If A = B and B = C in equilibrium, then A = C

### Heat Transfer
- **Heat (Q)**: Energy transferred due to temperature difference; joules (J) or calories (cal); 1 cal = 4.186 J
- **Specific Heat Capacity (c)**: Q = mcΔT; J/(kg·K)
- **Latent Heat**: Q = mL; phase change at constant T
  - **L_f**: Fusion (melting/freezing)
  - **L_v**: Vaporization (boiling/condensing)

### Mechanisms of Heat Transfer
- **Conduction**: Through material; molecular collisions
  - **Rate**: H = kA(T_H - T_C)/L; k = thermal conductivity; A = area; L = thickness
- **Convection**: Fluid motion; hot rises, cold sinks
- **Radiation**: Electromagnetic waves; no medium needed
  - **Stefan-Boltzmann Law**: P = εσAT⁴; ε = emissivity; σ = 5.67×10^{-8} W/(m²·K⁴)

### First Law of Thermodynamics
- **Energy Conservation**: ΔE_int = Q - W
- **Internal Energy (E_int)**: Total KE + PE of molecules; state function
- **Sign Convention**: Q > 0 (into system); W > 0 (by system)
- **Work**: W = ∫P dV; area under PV curve
- **Isothermal (ΔT = 0)**: ΔE_int = 0; Q = W
- **Adiabatic (Q = 0)**: ΔE_int = -W; no heat exchange
- **Isochoric (ΔV = 0)**: W = 0; ΔE_int = Q
- **Isobaric (ΔP = 0)**: W = PΔV

### Ideal Gas
- **Ideal Gas Law**: PV = nRT; R = 8.314 J/(mol·K)
- **Also**: PV = NkT; k = 1.38×10^{-23} J/K (Boltzmann constant); N = number of molecules
- **Kinetic Theory**: E_avg = (3/2)kT per molecule = (3/2)RT per mole
- **RMS Speed**: v_rms = √(3kT/m) = √(3RT/M); m = molecular mass; M = molar mass
- **Internal Energy**: E_int = (3/2)nRT (monatomic ideal gas)

### Heat Engines
- **Heat Engine**: Converts heat to work cyclically
- **Efficiency**: e = W/Q_H = (Q_H - Q_C)/Q_H; W = net work; Q_H = heat in; Q_C = heat out
- **e < 1 Always**: Cannot convert all heat to work (2nd Law)
- **Otto Cycle**: Gasoline engine; two adiabatic + two isochoric
- **Diesel Cycle**: Compression ignition; two adiabatic + isobaric + isochoric

### Second Law of Thermodynamics
- **Multiple Statements**:
  - Heat flows spontaneously from hot to cold (Clausius)
  - No 100% efficient heat engine (Kelvin-Planck)
  - Entropy of isolated system never decreases
- **Heat Engine Requirement**: Must exhaust heat to cold reservoir
- **Refrigerator/Heat Pump**: Moves heat from cold to hot; requires work

### Carnot Cycle
- **Most Efficient**: Reversible cycle between two temperatures
- **Four Steps**: Two isothermals + two adiabatics
- **Carnot Efficiency**: e_C = 1 - T_C/T_H; T in Kelvin; maximum possible
- **Real Engines**: e < e_C due to irreversibilities
- **Coefficient of Performance (COP)**:
  - Refrigerator: COP = Q_C/W
  - Heat Pump: COP = Q_H/W

### Entropy
- **Entropy (S)**: Measure of disorder; J/K; state function
- **Change**: ΔS = Q_rev/T (reversible process)
- **Second Law**: ΔS_universe ≥ 0; = 0 for reversible, > 0 for irreversible
- **Isolated System**: ΔS ≥ 0; entropy increases or stays constant
- **Reversible Process**: Infinitely slow; ΔS = 0 for universe
- **Irreversible Process**: Real processes; ΔS > 0 for universe
- **Statistical Interpretation**: S = k ln W; W = number of microstates
- **Third Law**: S → 0 as T → 0 K for perfect crystal

## Waves & Oscillations

### Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)
- **Restoring Force**: F = -kx; proportional to displacement; toward equilibrium
- **Acceleration**: a = -(k/m)x = -ω²x
- **Angular Frequency**: ω = √(k/m) = 2πf; rad/s
- **Period**: T = 2π/ω = 2π√(m/k); time for one cycle
- **Frequency**: f = 1/T = ω/(2π); Hz = 1/s
- **Position**: x(t) = A cos(ωt + φ); A = amplitude; φ = phase constant
- **Velocity**: v(t) = -Aω sin(ωt + φ); v_max = Aω at x = 0
- **Acceleration**: a(t) = -Aω² cos(ωt + φ); a_max = Aω² at x = ±A
- **Energy**: E = ½kA²; constant; KE + PE
  - KE = ½mv² = ½kA² sin²(ωt + φ)
  - PE = ½kx² = ½kA² cos²(ωt + φ)

### Pendulums
- **Simple Pendulum**: Mass on string; small angles
  - **Period**: T = 2π√(L/g); L = length; independent of mass/amplitude
  - **Frequency**: f = (1/2π)√(g/L)
- **Physical Pendulum**: Extended object; pivot not at CM
  - **Period**: T = 2π√(I/(mgd)); I = moment of inertia; d = distance pivot to CM

### Damped Oscillations
- **Damping Force**: F_d = -bv; opposes motion
- **Underdamped**: Oscillates with decreasing amplitude; x(t) = Ae^{-bt/(2m)} cos(ω't + φ)
- **Critically Damped**: Returns to equilibrium fastest without oscillating
- **Overdamped**: Returns slowly without oscillating

### Forced Oscillations and Resonance
- **Driving Force**: External periodic force; F = F₀ cos(ω_d t)
- **Steady State**: Oscillates at driving frequency ω_d
- **Resonance**: Maximum amplitude when ω_d ≈ ω₀ (natural frequency)
- **Amplitude**: Large at resonance; depends on damping
- **Applications**: Tuning circuits, musical instruments, bridges (avoid resonance)

### Wave Basics
- **Wave**: Disturbance that propagates; transfers energy, not matter
- **Mechanical Wave**: Requires medium (sound, water, string)
- **Electromagnetic Wave**: No medium needed (light, radio)
- **Transverse**: Oscillation perpendicular to propagation (light, string)
- **Longitudinal**: Oscillation parallel to propagation (sound)

### Wave Parameters
- **Amplitude (A)**: Maximum displacement
- **Wavelength (λ)**: Distance between repeats; meters
- **Period (T)**: Time for one cycle; seconds
- **Frequency (f)**: Cycles per second; Hz
- **Wave Speed**: v = fλ = λ/T
- **Wave Number**: k = 2π/λ; rad/m
- **Angular Frequency**: ω = 2πf; rad/s

### Wave Functions
- **Sinusoidal Wave**: y(x,t) = A sin(kx - ωt + φ) traveling right
  - Left: y(x,t) = A sin(kx + ωt + φ)
- **Phase**: (kx - ωt + φ); determines position in cycle
- **Phase Velocity**: v = ω/k
- **Particle Velocity**: ∂y/∂t

### Wave on String
- **Speed**: v = √(T/μ); T = tension; μ = linear mass density (kg/m)
- **Power**: P = ½μω²A²v; energy transfer rate
- **Reflection**: Fixed end inverts; free end doesn't
- **Transmission**: Partial reflection + transmission at boundary

### Sound Waves
- **Longitudinal**: Compressions and rarefactions
- **Speed in Air**: v ≈ 343 m/s at 20°C; v = √(γRT/M); increases with T
- **Intensity**: I = P/A; W/m²; power per area
- **Intensity Level**: β = 10 log(I/I₀); decibels (dB); I₀ = 10^{-12} W/m² (threshold)
- **Loudness Scale**: 0 dB (threshold), 60 dB (conversation), 120 dB (pain threshold)

### Doppler Effect
- **Frequency Shift**: Moving source/observer
- **Observer Moving**: f' = f(v ± v_o)/v; + approaching, - receding
- **Source Moving**: f' = fv/(v ∓ v_s); - approaching, + receding
- **Both Moving**: f' = f(v ± v_o)/(v ∓ v_s)
- **Shock Wave**: v_s > v; sonic boom; Mach cone

### Interference and Superposition
- **Principle of Superposition**: y_total = y₁ + y₂
- **Constructive Interference**: Crests align; amplitudes add
- **Destructive Interference**: Crest + trough; amplitudes subtract
- **Path Difference**: Δr = |r₂ - r₁|
  - Constructive: Δr = nλ; n = 0, 1, 2, ...
  - Destructive: Δr = (n + ½)λ
- **Beats**: Two close frequencies; f_beat = |f₁ - f₂|

### Standing Waves
- **Formation**: Interference of waves traveling opposite directions
- **Nodes**: Zero amplitude; fixed points; separation λ/2
- **Antinodes**: Maximum amplitude; midway between nodes
- **String Fixed Both Ends**: L = nλ/2; f_n = nv/(2L) = nf₁; n = 1, 2, 3, ...
  - f₁: Fundamental (1st harmonic)
  - f_n: nth harmonic = n × fundamental
- **Open Pipe (Both Ends)**: Same as string; L = nλ/2
- **Closed Pipe (One End)**: L = nλ/4; only odd harmonics; f_n = nv/(4L); n = 1, 3, 5, ...
