# General Chemistry Overview

## üéØ Purpose
This mindmap provides a comprehensive overview of college-level chemistry, covering atomic structure, chemical bonding, reactions, and the behavior of matter. It serves as a visual guide to understanding the relationships between chemical concepts and their practical applications.

## üìö Main Topics

### 1. Atomic Structure
The foundation of chemistry - understanding atoms:
- **Subatomic Particles**: Protons, neutrons, electrons
- **Electron Configuration**: Arrangement determining chemical behavior
- **Periodic Table**: Organization revealing patterns and properties
- **Periodic Trends**: Atomic radius, ionization energy, electronegativity

**Key Applications**: Materials science, spectroscopy, understanding chemical reactivity

### 2. Chemical Bonding
Forces holding matter together:
- **Ionic Bonding**: Electron transfer forming salts
- **Covalent Bonding**: Electron sharing in molecules
- **Molecular Geometry**: 3D shapes (VSEPR theory)
- **Intermolecular Forces**: Forces between molecules determining physical properties

**Key Applications**: Drug design, materials engineering, polymer science, nanotechnology

### 3. Stoichiometry
Quantitative relationships in chemistry:
- **Mole Concept**: Counting particles using Avogadro's number
- **Balancing Equations**: Mass conservation in reactions
- **Limiting Reactants**: Determining reaction yields
- **Percent Yield**: Measuring reaction efficiency

**Key Applications**: Manufacturing, quality control, pharmaceutical production, environmental analysis

### 4. States of Matter
Physical forms and phase transitions:
- **Solids**: Fixed structure and crystalline arrangements
- **Liquids**: Flowing matter with fixed volume
- **Gases**: Ideal and real gas laws
- **Phase Changes**: Energy in transitions

**Key Applications**: Materials processing, weather prediction, cryogenics, refrigeration

### 5. Thermochemistry
Energy in chemical reactions:
- **Enthalpy**: Heat content and reaction heat
- **Entropy**: Disorder and spontaneity
- **Gibbs Free Energy**: Predicting spontaneous reactions
- **Calorimetry**: Measuring heat changes

**Key Applications**: Energy storage, fuel efficiency, chemical manufacturing, climate science

### 6. Chemical Kinetics
Reaction rates and mechanisms:
- **Reaction Rates**: How fast reactions occur
- **Rate Laws**: Mathematical relationships
- **Activation Energy**: Energy barriers to reactions
- **Catalysis**: Speeding reactions without consumption

**Key Applications**: Industrial production, enzyme function, drug development, pollution control

### 7. Chemical Equilibrium
Dynamic balance in reactions:
- **Equilibrium Constant**: Quantifying equilibrium position
- **Le Ch√¢telier's Principle**: Predicting shifts
- **Solubility Equilibria**: Precipitation reactions
- **ICE Tables**: Problem-solving tool

**Key Applications**: Industrial chemistry, environmental chemistry, biochemical systems

### 8. Acids and Bases
Proton transfer chemistry:
- **Acid-Base Theories**: Arrhenius, Br√∏nsted-Lowry
- **pH Scale**: Measuring acidity
- **Buffers**: Resisting pH changes
- **Titrations**: Analytical technique

**Key Applications**: Medicine, biology, environmental science, food chemistry

### 9. Redox Reactions
Electron transfer processes:
- **Oxidation States**: Tracking electron transfer
- **Balancing Redox**: Half-reaction method
- **Electrochemistry**: Converting chemical to electrical energy
- **Batteries and Electrolysis**: Practical applications

**Key Applications**: Batteries, corrosion prevention, electroplating, energy storage

### 10. Organic Chemistry
Carbon-based molecules:
- **Hydrocarbons**: Alkanes, alkenes, alkynes, aromatics
- **Functional Groups**: Characteristic reactive groups
- **Isomers**: Same formula, different structures
- **Biological Molecules**: Carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, nucleic acids

**Key Applications**: Pharmaceuticals, plastics, fuels, biochemistry, materials

## üîó Interconnections

- Stoichiometry quantifies all chemical reactions

## üî¢ Key Concepts & Formulas

### Atomic Structure

**Subatomic Particles**
- Protons: +1 charge, mass ‚âà 1 amu
- Neutrons: 0 charge, mass ‚âà 1 amu
- Electrons: -1 charge, mass ‚âà 1/1836 amu

**Atomic Notation**: $^A_Z X$ where A = mass number, Z = atomic number
- **Isotopes**: Same Z, different A (different neutrons)

**Electron Configuration**
- **Aufbau principle**: Fill lowest energy first
- **Pauli exclusion**: Max 2 electrons per orbital (opposite spins)
- **Hund's rule**: Maximize unpaired electrons in degenerate orbitals
- **Notation**: 1s¬≤ 2s¬≤ 2p‚Å∂ 3s¬≤ 3p‚Å∂ 4s¬≤ 3d¬π‚Å∞...

**Periodic Trends**
- **Atomic radius**: Decreases across period (higher Z_eff), increases down group (more shells)
- **Ionization energy**: Increases across, decreases down (energy to remove electron)
- **Electronegativity**: Increases across, decreases down (attraction for electrons)
- **Electron affinity**: Generally more negative across period

### Chemical Bonding

**Ionic Bonding**
- Electron transfer: Metal ‚Üí nonmetal
- **Lattice energy**: Energy to separate ions, $U \propto \frac{Q_1Q_2}{r}$
- **Born-Haber cycle**: Relates formation enthalpy to lattice energy

**Covalent Bonding**
- Electron sharing between nonmetals
- **Bond order**: Single (1), double (2), triple (3)
- **Bond length**: Triple < double < single
- **Bond energy**: Triple > double > single
- **Formal charge**: $FC = V - N - \frac{B}{2}$ (V = valence, N = nonbonding, B = bonding)

**Molecular Geometry (VSEPR)**
- 2 domains: Linear (180¬∞)
- 3 domains: Trigonal planar (120¬∞)
- 4 domains: Tetrahedral (109.5¬∞)
- 5 domains: Trigonal bipyramidal (90¬∞, 120¬∞)
- 6 domains: Octahedral (90¬∞)

**Intermolecular Forces** (weakest to strongest)
1. London dispersion (all molecules)
2. Dipole-dipole (polar molecules)
3. Hydrogen bonding (H-F, H-O, H-N)
4. Ion-dipole (ions with polar molecules)

### Stoichiometry

**Mole Concept**
- **Avogadro's number**: $N_A = 6.022 \times 10^{23}$ particles/mol
- **Molar mass**: Grams per mole (from periodic table)
- **Conversions**: 
  - Moles = $\frac{\text{mass (g)}}{\text{molar mass}}$
  - Particles = moles √ó $N_A$

**Chemical Equations**
- **Balancing**: Equal atoms each side (conservation of mass)
- **Limiting reactant**: Consumed first, determines product amount
- **Theoretical yield**: Maximum product from stoichiometry
- **Percent yield**: $\frac{\text{actual}}{\text{theoretical}} \times 100\%$

**Empirical & Molecular Formulas**
- **Empirical**: Simplest whole number ratio
- **Molecular**: Actual number of atoms (multiple of empirical)

### States of Matter

**Gas Laws**
- **Boyle's law**: $P_1V_1 = P_2V_2$ (constant T, n)
- **Charles's law**: $\frac{V_1}{T_1} = \frac{V_2}{T_2}$ (constant P, n)
- **Avogadro's law**: $\frac{V_1}{n_1} = \frac{V_2}{n_2}$ (constant P, T)
- **Ideal gas law**: $PV = nRT$ ($R = 0.08206$ L¬∑atm/(mol¬∑K) or $8.314$ J/(mol¬∑K))
- **Dalton's law**: $P_{total} = \sum P_i$ (partial pressures)
- **Graham's law**: $\frac{r_1}{r_2} = \sqrt{\frac{M_2}{M_1}}$ (effusion rates)

**Kinetic Molecular Theory**
- $KE_{avg} = \frac{3}{2}RT$ (per mole) or $\frac{3}{2}k_BT$ (per molecule)
- $v_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}}$ (root-mean-square speed)

**Phase Changes**
- **Heat of fusion**: $q = m \cdot \Delta H_{fus}$ (solid ‚Üî liquid)
- **Heat of vaporization**: $q = m \cdot \Delta H_{vap}$ (liquid ‚Üî gas)
- **Clausius-Clapeyron**: $\ln\frac{P_2}{P_1} = -\frac{\Delta H_{vap}}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T_2} - \frac{1}{T_1}\right)$

**Solutions**
- **Molarity**: $M = \frac{\text{moles solute}}{\text{liters solution}}$
- **Molality**: $m = \frac{\text{moles solute}}{\text{kg solvent}}$
- **Mole fraction**: $\chi_A = \frac{n_A}{n_{total}}$
- **Dilution**: $M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$

**Colligative Properties** (depend on particle number, not identity)
- **Vapor pressure lowering**: $P_{solution} = \chi_{solvent} \cdot P¬∞_{solvent}$ (Raoult's law)
- **Boiling point elevation**: $\Delta T_b = K_b \cdot m \cdot i$
- **Freezing point depression**: $\Delta T_f = K_f \cdot m \cdot i$
- **Osmotic pressure**: $\Pi = MRT \cdot i$
- (i = van't Hoff factor)

### Thermochemistry

**Enthalpy (H)**
- **Exothermic**: $\Delta H < 0$ (releases heat)
- **Endothermic**: $\Delta H > 0$ (absorbs heat)
- **Standard enthalpy of formation**: $\Delta H¬∞_f$ (from elements at 25¬∞C, 1 atm)
- **Hess's law**: $\Delta H_{rxn} = \sum \Delta H¬∞_f(\text{products}) - \sum \Delta H¬∞_f(\text{reactants})$

**Calorimetry**
- $q = mc\Delta T$ (specific heat)
- $q = C\Delta T$ (heat capacity)

**Gibbs Free Energy**
- $\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$
- $\Delta G < 0$: Spontaneous
- $\Delta G > 0$: Non-spontaneous
- $\Delta G = 0$: Equilibrium
- **Standard**: $\Delta G¬∞ = -RT\ln K$
- **Non-standard**: $\Delta G = \Delta G¬∞ + RT\ln Q$

**Entropy (S)**
- Measure of disorder
- $\Delta S_{universe} > 0$ for spontaneous processes (Second Law)

### Chemical Kinetics

**Reaction Rate**
- **Average rate**: $-\frac{\Delta[\text{reactant}]}{\Delta t}$ or $\frac{\Delta[\text{product}]}{\Delta t}$
- **Instantaneous rate**: $\frac{d[\text{concentration}]}{dt}$

**Rate Laws**
- $\text{Rate} = k[A]^m[B]^n$
- k = rate constant (temperature dependent)
- m, n = reaction orders (determined experimentally)
- **Overall order**: m + n

**Integrated Rate Laws**
- **Zero order**: $[A] = [A]_0 - kt$, $t_{1/2} = \frac{[A]_0}{2k}$
- **First order**: $\ln[A] = \ln[A]_0 - kt$, $t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{k}$
- **Second order**: $\frac{1}{[A]} = \frac{1}{[A]_0} + kt$, $t_{1/2} = \frac{1}{k[A]_0}$

**Arrhenius Equation**
- $k = Ae^{-E_a/RT}$
- $\ln k = \ln A - \frac{E_a}{RT}$
- $\ln\frac{k_2}{k_1} = \frac{E_a}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{1}{T_2}\right)$

**Catalysts**
- Lower $E_a$, increase rate
- Not consumed in reaction
- Don't affect equilibrium position

### Chemical Equilibrium

**Equilibrium Constant**
- $K_c = \frac{[\text{products}]}{[\text{reactants}]}$ (concentration)
- $K_p = \frac{P_{\text{products}}}{P_{\text{reactants}}}$ (partial pressure)
- $K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n}$
- Large K: Products favored; Small K: Reactants favored

**Reaction Quotient (Q)**
- Same form as K, but not at equilibrium
- Q < K: Forward reaction
- Q > K: Reverse reaction
- Q = K: At equilibrium

**Le Ch√¢telier's Principle**
- Add reactant/remove product ‚Üí shift right
- Remove reactant/add product ‚Üí shift left
- Increase temperature: Endothermic direction
- Increase pressure: Fewer moles of gas side
- **Catalysts**: No effect on equilibrium position

**Solubility Equilibrium**
- $K_{sp} = [\text{cation}]^m[\text{anion}]^n$
- $Q_{sp} < K_{sp}$: Dissolves
- $Q_{sp} > K_{sp}$: Precipitates
- **Common ion effect**: Decreases solubility

### Acids & Bases

**Definitions**
- **Arrhenius**: Acids produce H‚Å∫, bases produce OH‚Åª
- **Br√∏nsted-Lowry**: Acids donate H‚Å∫, bases accept H‚Å∫
- **Lewis**: Acids accept electron pairs, bases donate electron pairs

**pH Scale**
- $\text{pH} = -\log[\text{H}^+]$
- $\text{pOH} = -\log[\text{OH}^-]$
- $\text{pH} + \text{pOH} = 14$ (at 25¬∞C)
- $[\text{H}^+][\text{OH}^-] = K_w = 1.0 \times 10^{-14}$ (at 25¬∞C)

**Acid-Base Strength**
- $K_a = \frac{[\text{H}^+][\text{A}^-]}{[\text{HA}]}$ (acid dissociation constant)
- $K_b = \frac{[\text{OH}^-][\text{BH}^+]}{[\text{B}]}$ (base dissociation constant)
- $K_a \times K_b = K_w$ (conjugate pair)
- $\text{pK}_a = -\log K_a$

**Buffer Solutions**
- Weak acid + conjugate base (or weak base + conjugate acid)
- **Henderson-Hasselbalch**: $\text{pH} = \text{pK}_a + \log\frac{[\text{A}^-]}{[\text{HA}]}$
- **Buffer capacity**: Maximum at pH = pK_a

**Titrations**
- **Equivalence point**: Moles acid = moles base
- **Endpoint**: Indicator color change
- Strong acid + strong base: pH = 7 at equivalence
- Weak acid + strong base: pH > 7 at equivalence
- Strong acid + weak base: pH < 7 at equivalence

### Redox & Electrochemistry

**Oxidation States**
- **Oxidation**: Loss of electrons (increase in oxidation state)
- **Reduction**: Gain of electrons (decrease in oxidation state)
- **OIL RIG**: Oxidation Is Loss, Reduction Is Gain

**Balancing Redox** (half-reaction method)
1. Separate into half-reactions
2. Balance atoms (except O and H)
3. Balance O with H‚ÇÇO
4. Balance H with H‚Å∫
5. Balance charge with electrons
6. Multiply to equalize electrons
7. Add half-reactions

**Electrochemical Cells**
- **Galvanic (voltaic)**: Spontaneous, generates electricity
  - Anode (oxidation, -) ‚Üí Cathode (reduction, +)
- **Electrolytic**: Non-spontaneous, requires electricity
  - Anode (+) ‚Üí Cathode (-)

**Cell Potential**
- $E¬∞_{cell} = E¬∞_{cathode} - E¬∞_{anode}$
- $\Delta G¬∞ = -nFE¬∞_{cell}$ (F = 96,485 C/mol)
- $E¬∞_{cell} = \frac{RT}{nF}\ln K = \frac{0.0592}{n}\log K$ (at 25¬∞C)

**Nernst Equation**
- $E_{cell} = E¬∞_{cell} - \frac{RT}{nF}\ln Q$
- $E_{cell} = E¬∞_{cell} - \frac{0.0592}{n}\log Q$ (at 25¬∞C)

### Organic Chemistry

**Hydrocarbons**
- **Alkanes**: C_nH_{2n+2} (single bonds, saturated)
- **Alkenes**: C_nH_{2n} (C=C double bonds)
- **Alkynes**: C_nH_{2n-2} (C‚â°C triple bonds)
- **Aromatics**: Benzene ring (C‚ÇÜH‚ÇÜ), resonance stabilized

**Functional Groups**
- **Alcohols**: R-OH (polar, H-bonding)
- **Aldehydes**: R-CHO (carbonyl at end)
- **Ketones**: R-CO-R' (carbonyl in middle)
- **Carboxylic acids**: R-COOH (acidic, H-bonding)
- **Esters**: R-COO-R' (pleasant odors)
- **Amines**: R-NH‚ÇÇ (basic)
- **Amides**: R-CO-NH‚ÇÇ (peptide bonds)

**Isomers**
- **Structural**: Different connectivity
- **Geometric**: Cis/trans (restricted rotation)
- **Optical**: Enantiomers (chiral, mirror images)

### Transition Elements

**Properties**
- Variable oxidation states
- Colored compounds (d-d transitions)
- Paramagnetic (unpaired d electrons)
- Catalytic activity

**Coordination Compounds**
- Metal center + ligands (electron donors)
- **Coordination number**: Typically 4 or 6
- **Crystal field theory**: d-orbital splitting explains color and magnetism

### Nuclear Chemistry

**Radioactive Decay**
- **Alpha (Œ±)**: $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A-4}_{Z-2}Y + ^4_2He$
- **Beta (Œ≤‚Åª)**: $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A}_{Z+1}Y + ^0_{-1}e + \bar{\nu}$
- **Gamma (Œ≥)**: High-energy photon, no change in A or Z
- **Positron (Œ≤‚Å∫)**: $^A_Z X \rightarrow ^{A}_{Z-1}Y + ^0_{+1}e + \nu$

**Decay Kinetics**
- $N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$
- $t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{\lambda}$

**Nuclear Reactions**
- **Fission**: Heavy nucleus splits ‚Üí energy (nuclear power)
- **Fusion**: Light nuclei combine ‚Üí energy (stars, sun)
- **Binding energy**: $BE = \Delta m \cdot c^2$ (mass defect)
