# Economics Terminology

## Core Concepts

### Microeconomics
- **Supply and Demand**: Fundamental market mechanism; equilibrium where supply meets demand
- **Elasticity**: Responsiveness of quantity to price change; elastic (|E| > 1), inelastic (|E| < 1)
- **Marginal Utility**: Additional satisfaction from consuming one more unit; diminishes with quantity
- **Opportunity Cost**: Value of the next best alternative foregone
- **Consumer Surplus**: Difference between willingness to pay and actual price paid
- **Producer Surplus**: Difference between market price and minimum acceptable price
- **Market Equilibrium**: Price where quantity demanded equals quantity supplied
- **Perfect Competition**: Many firms, identical products, free entry/exit, price takers
- **Monopoly**: Single seller; price maker; barriers to entry; deadweight loss
- **Oligopoly**: Few large firms; strategic interdependence; game theory applies

### Macroeconomics
- **GDP (Gross Domestic Product)**: Total market value of all final goods/services produced in a country in a year
- **Inflation**: Sustained increase in general price level; measured by CPI or GDP deflator
- **Unemployment**: Percentage of labor force actively seeking but unable to find work
- **Fiscal Policy**: Government spending and taxation to influence economy
- **Monetary Policy**: Central bank controls money supply and interest rates
- **Interest Rate**: Cost of borrowing money; set by central bank (federal funds rate)
- **Aggregate Demand**: Total demand for goods and services at each price level; AD = C + I + G + NX
- **Aggregate Supply**: Total output firms are willing to produce at each price level
- **Business Cycle**: Expansion, peak, contraction (recession), trough
- **Trade Balance**: Exports minus imports; surplus (positive) or deficit (negative)

### Finance
- **Present Value**: Current worth of future cash flows; PV = FV/(1+r)ⁿ
- **Compound Interest**: Interest earned on interest; A = P(1+r/n)^(nt)
- **Stocks**: Ownership shares in a company; dividends and capital gains
- **Bonds**: Debt instruments; fixed interest payments; face value at maturity
- **Diversification**: Spreading investments to reduce risk; don't put all eggs in one basket
- **Risk and Return**: Higher expected return requires higher risk; risk-return tradeoff
