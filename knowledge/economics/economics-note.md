## 📚 Main Topics

### 1. Microeconomics
Individual decision-making and market interactions.

- **Supply and Demand**:
  - Demand: quantity consumers willing to buy at each price; downward slope (law of demand)
  - Supply: quantity producers willing to sell; upward slope (law of supply)
  - Equilibrium: $Q_d = Q_s$; market-clearing price and quantity
  - Shifts: income, preferences, prices of related goods (demand); technology, input costs (supply)
  - Consumer surplus: area below demand, above price; producer surplus: above supply, below price
  - Deadweight loss: inefficiency from taxes, price controls, monopoly

- **Elasticity**:
  - Price elasticity of demand: $E_d = \frac{\%\Delta Q_d}{\%\Delta P}$
  - Elastic |E| > 1 (luxury); inelastic |E| < 1 (necessity); unit elastic |E| = 1
  - Income elasticity: normal goods > 0; inferior goods < 0
  - Cross-price: substitutes > 0; complements < 0
  - Determinants: substitutes available, necessity vs luxury, time horizon, budget share

- **Consumer Theory**:
  - Utility: satisfaction from consumption; diminishing marginal utility
  - Budget constraint: $P_xX + P_yY = I$
  - Optimal choice: $MRS = \frac{MU_x}{MU_y} = \frac{P_x}{P_y}$ (tangency of indifference curve and budget line)
  - Income effect: change in purchasing power; substitution effect: relative price change
  - Giffen goods: positive price-quantity relationship (theoretical)

- **Production and Costs**:
  - Production function: $Q = f(L, K)$; short run (one input fixed) vs long run (all variable)
  - Marginal product: $MP_L = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta L}$; diminishing marginal returns
  - Costs: TC = FC + VC; AC = TC/Q; MC = dTC/dQ
  - Economies of scale: AC decreases as Q increases; diseconomies: AC increases
  - Profit maximization: produce where MR = MC

- **Market Structures**:
  - **Perfect competition**: many firms, identical product, free entry/exit, price takers; P = MC = MR
  - **Monopoly**: single seller, barriers to entry, price maker; MR < P; deadweight loss; natural monopoly: AC falls over relevant range
  - **Monopolistic competition**: many firms, differentiated products, free entry; short-run profit → long-run zero profit; excess capacity
  - **Oligopoly**: few firms, interdependent; game theory; Cournot (quantity), Bertrand (price), Stackelberg (leader-follower); cartels (collude → monopoly outcome)

- **Market Failures & Government**:
  - Externalities: positive (education) or negative (pollution); Pigouvian tax/subsidy
  - Public goods: non-rival, non-excludable; free-rider problem
  - Common resources: rival but non-excludable; tragedy of the commons
  - Asymmetric information: adverse selection (hidden type), moral hazard (hidden action)
  - Government intervention: taxes, subsidies, regulation, price controls (ceiling/floor)

### 2. Macroeconomics
Economy-wide phenomena: growth, inflation, unemployment, policy.

- **National Income Accounting**:
  - GDP (expenditure): $Y = C + I + G + NX$
  - GDP (income): wages + rent + interest + profit
  - Real GDP = Nominal GDP / Price Index × 100
  - GDP per capita: Y/N; growth rate: $(Y_t - Y_{t-1})/Y_{t-1}$
  - GNP vs GDP; flow vs stock variables

- **Aggregate Demand & Supply**:
  - AD: total demand at each price level; slopes down (wealth, interest rate, exchange rate effects)
  - SRAS: short-run supply; upward due to sticky wages/prices
  - LRAS: long-run at potential output; vertical (classical)
  - AD shifts: fiscal/monetary policy, expectations, wealth, foreign demand
  - AS shifts: input costs, technology, supply shocks

- **Inflation**:
  - CPI: cost of fixed basket; GDP deflator: ratio nominal/real GDP
  - Demand-pull: too much money chasing too few goods
  - Cost-push: rising input costs → higher prices
  - Quantity theory: MV = PY; Fisher equation: $r \approx i - \pi$
  - Costs of inflation: shoe-leather, menu costs, uncertainty, redistribution

- **Unemployment**:
  - Types: frictional (between jobs), structural (skills mismatch), cyclical (recession)
  - Natural rate: frictional + structural; NAIRU
  - Okun's Law: 1% above natural → ~2% below potential GDP
  - Phillips curve: short-run tradeoff π vs u; long-run vertical at uₙ

- **Fiscal Policy**:
  - Government spending (G) and taxation (T)
  - Expansionary: ↑G or ↓T → ↑AD; contractionary: ↓G or ↑T → ↓AD
  - Multiplier: $k = 1/(1-MPC)$; tax multiplier: $-MPC/(1-MPC)$
  - Automatic stabilizers: progressive taxes, unemployment benefits
  - Budget deficit: G > T; national debt: accumulated deficits; debt/GDP ratio
  - Crowding out: government borrowing raises interest rates → reduces private investment

- **Monetary Policy**:
  - Central bank (Fed, ECB, BOK) controls money supply and interest rates
  - Tools: open market operations, discount rate, reserve requirements, quantitative easing
  - Expansionary: ↑M → ↓i → ↑I → ↑AD; contractionary: opposite
  - Money multiplier: $m = 1/rr$
  - Taylor Rule: $i = r^* + \pi + 0.5(\pi - \pi^*) + 0.5(y - y^*)$
  - IS-LM model: goods market (IS) + money market (LM) → equilibrium (Y, r)

- **Economic Growth**:
  - Solow model: $\Delta k = sf(k) - (\delta + n + g)k$; steady state: $sf(k^*) = (\delta + n + g)k^*$
  - Sources: capital accumulation, labor force growth, technology (TFP)
  - Golden rule: $f'(k^*) = \delta + n + g$ (max consumption)
  - Convergence: poor countries grow faster (conditional convergence)
  - Endogenous growth: R&D, human capital, knowledge spillovers

### 3. International Economics
Trade, exchange rates, and global financial flows.

- **Trade Theory**:
  - Absolute advantage (Adam Smith): produce at lower absolute cost
  - Comparative advantage (Ricardo): produce at lower opportunity cost; basis for trade gains
  - Heckscher-Ohlin: countries export goods intensive in their abundant factor
  - Terms of trade: export price / import price
- **Trade Policy**:
  - Free trade vs protectionism; tariffs, quotas, subsidies
  - WTO, bilateral/multilateral agreements
  - Arguments for protection: infant industry, national security, anti-dumping
- **Exchange Rates**:
  - Nominal: domestic/foreign currency ratio; real: adjusted for price levels
  - Fixed vs floating regimes; managed float
  - PPP: exchange rate adjusts so identical goods cost same across countries
  - Interest rate parity: $(1+i_d) = (F/S)(1+i_f)$
- **Balance of Payments**: Current account (trade, services, transfers) + capital/financial account = 0

### 4. Finance
Management of money, investments, and risk.

- **Time Value of Money**: FV = PV(1+r)ⁿ; PV = FV/(1+r)ⁿ
  - Annuity PV: $PMT \cdot \frac{1-(1+r)^{-n}}{r}$; Perpetuity: PMT/r
  - Compound interest: $A = P(1+r/n)^{nt}$; continuous: $A = Pe^{rt}$
  - Rule of 72: doubling time ≈ 72/r%

- **Investment Valuation**:
  - Bonds: $P = \sum \frac{C}{(1+r)^t} + \frac{F}{(1+r)^n}$; yield to maturity
  - Stocks: Gordon growth: $P_0 = D_1/(r-g)$; P/E ratio
  - CAPM: $E(R_i) = R_f + \beta_i[E(R_m) - R_f]$
  - Efficient market hypothesis: prices reflect all available information (weak/semi-strong/strong)

- **Portfolio Theory**:
  - Diversification reduces unsystematic risk
  - Portfolio return: $E(R_p) = \sum w_i E(R_i)$
  - Portfolio risk: depends on covariances; $\sigma_p^2 = \sum\sum w_iw_j\sigma_i\sigma_j\rho_{ij}$
  - Sharpe ratio: $(E(R_p) - R_f)/\sigma_p$; higher is better
  - Efficient frontier: maximum return for given risk

- **Banking & Financial System**:
  - Commercial banks: accept deposits, make loans; fractional reserve banking
  - Investment banks: underwriting, M&A, trading
  - Central bank: lender of last resort; monetary policy
  - Systemic risk: failure of one institution affects entire system

### 5. Behavioral Economics
Psychology of economic decision-making.

- **Bounded Rationality**: Limited information-processing; satisficing vs optimizing
- **Prospect Theory (Kahneman & Tversky)**: Loss aversion (losses weigh ~2× gains); reference-dependent; probability weighting (overweight small probabilities)
- **Cognitive Biases**: Anchoring, availability heuristic, confirmation bias, status quo bias, framing effects, endowment effect, sunk cost fallacy
- **Time Inconsistency**: Present bias; hyperbolic discounting; preference reversal over time
- **Nudges (Thaler & Sunstein)**: Choice architecture; default options; opt-out vs opt-in
- **Social Preferences**: Fairness, reciprocity, altruism; ultimatum and dictator games
- **Market Anomalies**: Overreaction, momentum, calendar effects; challenges to EMH
