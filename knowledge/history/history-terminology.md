# History Terminology

## Core Concepts

### Ancient Civilizations
- **Mesopotamia**: Cradle of civilization; Sumer, Babylon, Assyria; cuneiform writing; Code of Hammurabi
- **Ancient Egypt**: Nile-based civilization; pharaohs, pyramids, hieroglyphics; 3000+ years of continuity
- **Ancient Greece**: Democracy (Athens), philosophy (Socrates, Plato, Aristotle), Olympic Games, city-states (polis)
- **Roman Empire**: Republic to Empire; Pax Romana; law, engineering (roads, aqueducts); fall in 476 CE
- **Ancient China**: Dynasties; Confucianism, Taoism; Great Wall, Silk Road; paper, printing, gunpowder, compass
- **Ancient India**: Indus Valley civilization; Hinduism, Buddhism; Maurya and Gupta empires; zero and decimal system

### Medieval Period
- **Feudalism**: Political system based on land ownership; lords, vassals, serfs
- **Byzantine Empire**: Eastern Roman Empire; Constantinople; preserved Greek/Roman knowledge; fell 1453
- **Islamic Golden Age**: 8th-14th centuries; advances in mathematics, astronomy, medicine; House of Wisdom
- **Crusades**: Religious wars (1095-1291); Christian attempts to reclaim Holy Land
- **Magna Carta (1215)**: Limited English king's power; foundation for constitutional law
- **Black Death (1347-1351)**: Bubonic plague killed ~30-60% of European population; social upheaval

### Renaissance and Early Modern
- **Renaissance**: Cultural rebirth 14th-17th century; humanism; art (da Vinci, Michelangelo); science
- **Reformation**: Martin Luther (1517); Protestant churches; Counter-Reformation
- **Scientific Revolution**: Copernicus, Galileo, Newton; empirical method; heliocentric model
- **Age of Exploration**: Columbus (1492), Magellan; colonialism; Columbian Exchange
- **Enlightenment**: 18th century; reason, individual rights; Locke, Voltaire, Rousseau, Montesquieu

### Revolutions and Modern Era
- **American Revolution (1776)**: Independence from Britain; Constitution; Bill of Rights
- **French Revolution (1789)**: Overthrow of monarchy; liberty, equality, fraternity; Reign of Terror
- **Industrial Revolution**: 18th-19th century; mechanization, steam power, factories; urbanization
- **World War I (1914-1918)**: Alliance systems, trench warfare; Treaty of Versailles
- **World War II (1939-1945)**: Axis vs Allies; Holocaust; atomic bombs; United Nations founded
- **Cold War (1947-1991)**: US vs USSR; nuclear arms race; proxy wars; Berlin Wall; Soviet collapse
- **Decolonization**: Post-WWII independence movements in Asia and Africa; end of European empires

### Asian History
- **Chinese Dynasties**: Qin (unification), Han (Silk Road), Tang (golden age), Song (technology), Ming (exploration), Qing (last dynasty)
- **Japanese History**: Feudal Japan (samurai, shogunate); Meiji Restoration (1868); modernization; WWII
- **Korean History**: Three Kingdoms; Joseon Dynasty; Japanese occupation (1910-1945); Korean War (1950-1953); division into North and South
- **Indian History**: Mughal Empire; British colonial rule; independence movement (Gandhi, 1947); partition
