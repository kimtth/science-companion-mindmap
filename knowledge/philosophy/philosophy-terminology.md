# Philosophy Terminology

## Core Concepts

### Logic and Argumentation
- **Argument**: Premises leading to a conclusion; valid if conclusion follows from premises
- **Deductive Reasoning**: General to specific; if premises true and valid, conclusion necessarily true
- **Inductive Reasoning**: Specific to general; conclusion probable but not certain
- **Fallacy**: Error in reasoning; formal (structural) or informal (content)
- **Syllogism**: Deductive argument with major premise, minor premise, conclusion
- **Validity**: Conclusion follows logically from premises (regardless of truth of premises)
- **Soundness**: Valid argument with all true premises

### Ethics
- **Virtue Ethics (Aristotle)**: Character-based; cultivating virtues (courage, temperance, justice, wisdom)
- **Deontological Ethics (Kant)**: Duty-based; categorical imperative — act only according to rules you could will universal
- **Utilitarianism (Mill, Bentham)**: Consequence-based; maximize happiness/utility for greatest number
- **Social Contract (Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau)**: Morality from agreement among individuals to form society
- **Ethics of Care**: Emphasis on relationships and responsibility; feminist ethics tradition
- **Moral Relativism**: Moral judgments are relative to culture or individual; no universal moral truths
- **Moral Realism**: Objective moral facts exist independent of belief or culture

### Epistemology
- **Knowledge**: Traditionally justified true belief; Gettier problem challenges this definition
- **Empiricism**: Knowledge comes from sensory experience (Locke, Hume)
- **Rationalism**: Knowledge comes from reason independent of experience (Descartes, Leibniz)
- **Skepticism**: Doubting claims to knowledge; radical skepticism questions all knowledge
- **A Priori**: Knowledge independent of experience (mathematics, logic)
- **A Posteriori**: Knowledge dependent on experience (empirical observation)

### Metaphysics
- **Ontology**: Study of what exists; being and reality
- **Dualism (Descartes)**: Mind and body are distinct substances
- **Materialism/Physicalism**: Only physical matter exists; mind is physical process
- **Free Will vs Determinism**: Whether choices are genuinely free or determined by prior causes
- **Personal Identity**: What makes a person the same over time; memory, body, psychological continuity
- **Causation**: One event bringing about another; Hume's problem of induction

### Political Philosophy
- **Liberalism**: Individual rights and freedoms; limited government; rule of law
- **Conservatism**: Tradition, social stability, gradual change
- **Social Justice (Rawls)**: Justice as fairness; veil of ignorance; difference principle
- **Libertarianism**: Maximum individual liberty; minimal state intervention
- **Marxism**: Class struggle; critique of capitalism; means of production
