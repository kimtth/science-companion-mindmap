## 📚 Main Topics

### 1. Biological Bases of Behavior
The neural and physiological foundations of thought and action.

- **Neurons**:
  - Structure: dendrites (receive), soma (cell body), axon (transmit), terminal buttons (release)
  - Action potential: resting (-70mV) → threshold → depolarization → repolarization → refractory
  - All-or-none principle: fires fully or not at all
  - Myelin sheath: insulation; speeds transmission; nodes of Ranvier for saltatory conduction
  - Synapse: axon terminal → synaptic cleft → postsynaptic dendrite
- **Neurotransmitters**:
  - Acetylcholine (ACh): muscle contraction, attention, memory
  - Dopamine: reward, motivation, movement; deficit → Parkinson's; excess → schizophrenia
  - Serotonin: mood, sleep, appetite; deficit → depression
  - GABA: primary inhibitory; reduces neural activity; anxiety drugs target GABA receptors
  - Glutamate: primary excitatory; learning and memory
  - Norepinephrine: alertness, arousal; fight-or-flight
  - Endorphins: natural painkillers; runner's high
- **Brain Structure**:
  - **Hindbrain**: Medulla (vital functions), Pons (sleep/arousal), Cerebellum (coordination, balance)
  - **Midbrain**: Superior/inferior colliculi; reticular activating system (arousal, attention)
  - **Forebrain**:
    - Thalamus: sensory relay station (except smell)
    - Hypothalamus: homeostasis, hunger, thirst, temperature, hormones (pituitary control)
    - Hippocampus: memory formation (episodic/spatial); H.M. case
    - Amygdala: emotion processing, fear conditioning
    - Basal ganglia: voluntary movement, procedural learning, habit formation
  - **Cerebral Cortex** (lobes):
    - Frontal: planning, decision-making, personality; motor cortex; Broca's area (speech production)
    - Parietal: somatosensory processing; spatial awareness
    - Temporal: auditory processing; Wernicke's area (language comprehension); face recognition
    - Occipital: visual processing
  - **Hemispheric lateralization**: Left (language, logic, sequential), Right (spatial, holistic, creative); corpus callosum connects
- **Endocrine System**: Hormones via bloodstream; slower but longer-lasting than neural
  - Pituitary: master gland; growth hormone, ACTH, oxytocin
  - Adrenals: cortisol (stress), epinephrine/norepinephrine (fight-or-flight)
  - Thyroid: metabolism regulation
- **Neuroplasticity**: Brain's ability to reorganize; critical periods; experience-dependent

### 2. Cognitive Psychology
Mental processes: attention, memory, language, problem-solving, decision-making.

- **Memory**:
  - **Sensory memory**: Brief (iconic ~500ms, echoic ~3-4s); large capacity
  - **Short-term/Working memory**: Limited capacity (Miller's 7±2 items); ~20-30 seconds without rehearsal
    - Baddeley model: central executive, phonological loop, visuospatial sketchpad, episodic buffer
  - **Long-term memory**: Unlimited capacity; potentially permanent
    - Explicit (declarative): episodic (personal events) + semantic (facts, general knowledge)
    - Implicit (non-declarative): procedural (skills), priming, conditioning
  - **Encoding**: Levels of processing (Craik & Lockhart); deep (meaning) > shallow (appearance)
    - Elaborative rehearsal > maintenance rehearsal; schemas aid encoding
  - **Retrieval**: Recognition (easier) vs recall (harder); context-dependent; state-dependent
    - Retrieval cues; encoding specificity principle (Tulving)
  - **Forgetting**: Decay theory; interference (proactive: old→new, retroactive: new→old); retrieval failure
    - Ebbinghaus forgetting curve: rapid initial forgetting, then slows
  - **Memory distortion**: Misinformation effect (Loftus); source monitoring errors; false memories
- **Attention**:
  - Selective: focus on specific input; cocktail party effect; filter theories (Broadbent, Treisman)
  - Divided: multitasking; limited capacity; automatic vs controlled processing
  - Change blindness; inattentional blindness (Simons & Chabris gorilla study)
- **Language**:
  - Phonemes → morphemes → syntax → semantics → pragmatics
  - Chomsky: universal grammar; LAD (Language Acquisition Device); generative grammar
  - Sapir-Whorf hypothesis: language influences thought (strong/weak version)
  - Critical period: before puberty for native-like acquisition (Lenneberg)
- **Problem Solving & Decision Making**:
  - Algorithms (systematic) vs heuristics (shortcuts)
  - Availability heuristic: judge frequency by ease of recall
  - Representativeness heuristic: judge probability by similarity to prototype
  - Anchoring: initial information biases estimates
  - Confirmation bias: seek information confirming existing beliefs
  - Framing effect: presentation affects choice
  - Dual-process theory (Kahneman): System 1 (fast, intuitive) vs System 2 (slow, deliberate)

### 3. Developmental Psychology
Changes across the lifespan.

- **Piaget's Cognitive Stages**:
  - Sensorimotor (0-2): Object permanence; means-ends behavior
  - Preoperational (2-7): Symbolic thought; egocentrism; lack of conservation
  - Concrete operational (7-11): Conservation; logical operations; classification
  - Formal operational (11+): Abstract reasoning; hypothetical-deductive thought
- **Vygotsky**: Zone of proximal development (ZPD); scaffolding; social construction of knowledge; more knowledgeable other (MKO)
- **Erikson's Psychosocial Stages** (8 stages, lifespan):
  1. Trust vs Mistrust (0-1): Caregiver reliability
  2. Autonomy vs Shame (1-3): Self-control, independence
  3. Initiative vs Guilt (3-6): Purpose, exploration
  4. Industry vs Inferiority (6-12): Competence, academic skills
  5. Identity vs Role Confusion (12-18): Self-concept, identity formation
  6. Intimacy vs Isolation (18-40): Close relationships
  7. Generativity vs Stagnation (40-65): Contributing to society
  8. Integrity vs Despair (65+): Life reflection, acceptance
- **Attachment (Bowlby/Ainsworth)**:
  - Secure: confident exploration; responsive caregiver
  - Anxious-ambivalent: clingy; inconsistent caregiver
  - Avoidant: independent; dismissive caregiver
  - Disorganized: confused; frightening caregiver (abuse/neglect)
  - Strange Situation procedure (Ainsworth)
- **Kohlberg's Moral Development**: Pre-conventional (punishment/reward), conventional (social norms/law), post-conventional (universal principles)
- **Nature vs Nurture**: Gene-environment interaction; epigenetics; twin studies; heritability estimates

### 4. Social Psychology
How people think about, influence, and relate to others.

- **Social Cognition**:
  - Attribution theory: Internal (dispositional) vs external (situational) causes
  - Fundamental attribution error (FAE): overweight disposition for others' behavior
  - Self-serving bias: attribute success internally, failure externally
  - Cognitive dissonance (Festinger): discomfort from inconsistent beliefs/actions → change beliefs
  - Attitudes: affective, behavioral, cognitive (ABC model)
  - Persuasion: Elaboration Likelihood Model — central route (logic) vs peripheral route (cues)
- **Social Influence**:
  - Conformity (Asch): match group behavior; informational (what's correct?) vs normative (social approval)
  - Obedience (Milgram): follow authority orders; 65% administered maximum "shock"
  - Stanford Prison (Zimbardo): role and situation overpower individual disposition
  - Group polarization: groups make more extreme decisions than individuals
  - Groupthink (Janis): consensus-seeking suppresses critical thinking; illusion of invulnerability
  - Social facilitation: better performance on easy tasks; worse on hard tasks (when observed)
  - Social loafing: reduced effort in groups; diffusion of responsibility
- **Prosocial Behavior**:
  - Bystander effect: more witnesses → less likely to help; diffusion of responsibility
  - Altruism: helping without expectation of reward; kin selection, reciprocal altruism
- **Prejudice & Discrimination**:
  - Stereotypes (cognitive), prejudice (affective), discrimination (behavioral)
  - Social identity theory (Tajfel): in-group bias; minimal group paradigm
  - Contact hypothesis (Allport): equal-status contact reduces prejudice
  - Implicit bias: unconscious associations; IAT (Implicit Association Test)
- **Aggression**: Biological (testosterone, serotonin), frustration-aggression hypothesis, social learning (Bandura), deindividuation

### 5. Abnormal Psychology
Understanding, classifying, and treating mental disorders.

- **Classification**: DSM-5 (categorical); ICD-11 (WHO); biopsychosocial model
- **Anxiety Disorders**:
  - Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD): chronic, excessive worry
  - Panic disorder: sudden attacks; agoraphobia
  - Specific phobias: intense fear of specific objects/situations
  - Social anxiety: fear of social judgment
  - OCD: obsessions (intrusive thoughts) + compulsions (repetitive behaviors)
- **Mood Disorders**:
  - Major depressive disorder: persistent sadness, anhedonia, fatigue, sleep/appetite changes; serotonin hypothesis
  - Bipolar disorder: alternating mania (elevated mood, risk-taking) and depression
  - Seasonal affective disorder: depression linked to reduced daylight
- **Schizophrenia**: Psychotic disorder; positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions) + negative symptoms (flat affect, social withdrawal) + cognitive symptoms (disorganized thought); dopamine hypothesis
- **Personality Disorders**:
  - Cluster A (odd/eccentric): paranoid, schizoid, schizotypal
  - Cluster B (dramatic): antisocial (ASPD), borderline (BPD), histrionic, narcissistic
  - Cluster C (anxious): avoidant, dependent, obsessive-compulsive PD
- **Treatment Approaches**:
  - **Psychotherapy**: CBT (cognitive-behavioral), psychodynamic, humanistic (Rogers), behavioral, DBT, EMDR
  - **Biomedical**: Antidepressants (SSRIs, SNRIs), antipsychotics, anxiolytics (benzodiazepines), mood stabilizers (lithium)
  - **Eclectic/integrative**: combining approaches; evidence-based practice

### 6. Learning & Conditioning
How organisms acquire new behaviors.

- **Classical Conditioning (Pavlov)**:
  - UCS (food) → UCR (salivation); CS (bell) paired with UCS → CR (salivation to bell)
  - Acquisition, extinction, spontaneous recovery, stimulus generalization, stimulus discrimination
  - Garcia effect: taste aversion; biological preparedness
  - Little Albert (Watson): conditioned fear response
- **Operant Conditioning (Skinner)**:
  - Reinforcement: positive (add pleasant) or negative (remove unpleasant) → increases behavior
  - Punishment: positive (add unpleasant) or negative (remove pleasant) → decreases behavior
  - Schedules: fixed-ratio, variable-ratio (highest response rate), fixed-interval, variable-interval
  - Shaping: reinforce successive approximations
  - Thorndike's law of effect: satisfying consequences strengthen behavior
- **Observational Learning (Bandura)**:
  - Bobo doll experiment: children imitate aggressive models
  - Modeling: attention → retention → reproduction → motivation
  - Vicarious reinforcement: learning from others' consequences
- **Cognitive Learning**: Insight learning (Köhler), latent learning (Tolman), cognitive maps

### 7. Sensation & Perception
How we detect and interpret sensory information.

- **Sensation**: Detection of physical stimuli by sensory receptors
  - Absolute threshold: minimum stimulation detected 50% of time
  - Difference threshold (JND): smallest detectable difference; Weber's Law: $\Delta I/I = k$
  - Signal detection theory: response depends on sensitivity + response criterion (hits, misses, false alarms, correct rejections)
  - Sensory adaptation: decreased sensitivity to constant stimulation
- **Vision**: Rods (dim light, periphery), cones (color, acuity, fovea)
  - Trichromatic theory (Young-Helmholtz): 3 cone types (R, G, B)
  - Opponent-process theory (Hering): opposing color pairs
  - Feature detection: specific cortical neurons for edges, lines, angles (Hubel & Wiesel)
- **Hearing**: Sound waves → outer/middle/inner ear → basilar membrane → auditory nerve
  - Place theory: high frequencies; temporal theory: low frequencies; volley principle: medium
- **Gestalt Principles**: Figure-ground, proximity, similarity, closure, continuity, common fate
  - Perception organizes raw sensation into meaningful patterns
- **Depth Perception**: Binocular (retinal disparity, convergence) + monocular (relative size, linear perspective, interposition, texture gradient)

### 8. Personality Theories
Enduring patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaving.

- **Psychoanalytic (Freud)**: Id (pleasure principle), ego (reality principle), superego (morality)
  - Psychosexual stages: oral, anal, phallic (Oedipus/Electra), latency, genital
  - Defense mechanisms: repression, denial, projection, displacement, sublimation, rationalization
- **Neo-Freudian**: Jung (collective unconscious, archetypes), Adler (inferiority complex, striving for superiority), Horney (basic anxiety, criticized penis envy)
- **Humanistic**:
  - Maslow: hierarchy of needs (physiological → safety → belonging → esteem → self-actualization)
  - Rogers: unconditional positive regard; self-concept; fully functioning person
- **Trait Theories**:
  - Big Five (OCEAN): Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism
  - Allport: cardinal, central, secondary traits
  - Cattell: 16 personality factors
  - Eysenck: extraversion, neuroticism, psychoticism
- **Social-Cognitive (Bandura)**: Reciprocal determinism — behavior, cognition, environment interact
  - Self-efficacy: belief in one's capability; strongest predictor of behavior change
- **Assessment**: Self-report (NEO-PI-R, MMPI-2), projective (Rorschach, TAT), behavioral observation

### 9. Research Methods
Scientific approaches to studying behavior.

- **Scientific Method**: Observation → hypothesis → experiment → analysis → conclusion
- **Experimental Design**: Independent variable (manipulated), dependent variable (measured), control group
  - Random assignment: reduces confounds; double-blind: reduces bias
- **Correlational Studies**: Measure association; correlation ≠ causation; $r = -1$ to $+1$
- **Descriptive Methods**: Case study, naturalistic observation, survey
- **Ethics**: Informed consent, debriefing, confidentiality, no harm; IRB approval; APA guidelines
- **Statistical Concepts**: Mean, median, mode; standard deviation; p-value; effect size; statistical significance (p < .05)
